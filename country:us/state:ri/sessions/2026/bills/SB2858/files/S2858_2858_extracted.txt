A N A C T
RELATING TO BUSINESSES AND PROFESSIONS -- EYELASH SERVICES LICENSING
AND REGULATION ACT
Introduced By: Senators Vargas, Quezada, Lauria, Murray, Valverde, DiPalma, DiMario,
Kallman, and Mack
Date Introduced: March 04, 2026
Referred To: Senate Health & Human Services
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Findings. The General Assembly finds and declares:
2 (1) There is a significant regulatory gap in the oversight of eyelash services, including the
3 application, removal, and add-on of eyelash extensions, as well as lash lifts and tinting.
4 (2) The Rhode Island department of health receives in excess of one hundred fifty (150)
5 complaints annually relating to cosmetology establishments, with approximately ninety percent
6 (90%) concerning eyelash services, including improper adhesive use, infections, and vision-related
7 injuries.
8 (3) The department currently maintains limited inspection capacity relative to the number
9 of licensed establishments statewide.
10 (4) The purpose of this chapter is to protect public health and safety by establishing
11 licensure standards, business permitting requirements, inspection authority, and enforcement
12 mechanisms for eyelash services.
13 SECTION 2. Title 5 of the General Laws entitled "BUSINESSES AND PROFESSIONS"
14 is hereby amended by adding thereto the following chapter:
15 CHAPTER 97
16 EYELASH SERVICES LICENSING AND REGULATION ACT
17 5-97-1. Short title.
18 This chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Eyelash Services Licensing and

1 Regulation Act."
2 5-97-2. Definitions.
3 As used in this chapter, the following terms have the following meanings:
4 (1) "Department" means the Rhode Island department of health.
5 (2) "Division" means the division of professional regulation within the department of labor
6 and training.
7 (3) "Establishment" means any retail shop, salon, or facility where eyelash services are
8 provided to the public.
9 (4) "Eyelash services" means the application, removal, and add-on of eyelash extensions,
10 as well as lash lifts and eyelash tinting.
11 (5) "Eyelash service technician" means a person licensed pursuant to this chapter to provide
12 eyelash services.
13 5-97-3. License required for eyelash service technicians.
14 (a) No person shall engage in the practice of eyelash services for compensation unless
15 licensed as an eyelash service technician pursuant to this chapter.
16 (b) An applicant for licensure shall:
17 (1) Submit an application to the division on a form prescribed by the division;
18 (2) Pay an application fee of one hundred dollars ($100);
19 (3) Provide evidence of completion of not less than fifty (50) hours of instruction in eyelash
20 services at a cosmetology school approved by the state;
21 (4) Successfully pass a written examination and a practical examination administered or
22 approved by the State of Rhode Island; and
23 (5) Meet such additional education, training, and competency standards as may be
24 established by regulation.
25 (c) A license issued under this chapter shall authorize the individual to perform only
26 eyelash services and shall not authorize the practice of cosmetology, esthetics, or any other licensed
27 profession.
28 5-97-4. Establishment license and permit required.
29 (a) Any establishment offering eyelash services shall obtain:
30 (1) A retail license issued by the appropriate municipal authority, if required; and
31 (2) A permit and license to operate issued by the department.
32 (b) The licensed technician and/or business owner operating the establishment shall ensure
33 that the establishment complies with all health, sanitation, and safety standards established by rules
34 and regulations promulgated by the department or the division.

1 (c) No establishment shall employ or permit an unlicensed individual to perform eyelash
2 services.
3 5-97-5. Regulatory authority.
4 (a) The department shall have authority to promulgate rules and regulations necessary to
5 implement and enforce the provisions of this chapter including, but not limited to, standards for
6 sanitation, infection control, adhesive use, ventilation, recordkeeping, and consumer protection.
7 (b) The department may conduct inspections of licensed establishments and shall
8 investigate complaints.
9 (c) The department is authorized to assess administrative fines for violations of this chapter
10 or any rules or regulations promulgated pursuant to this section.
11 (d) Revenue derived from fines and fees under this chapter shall be deposited into a
12 restricted receipt account within the department to support enforcement activities, including
13 inspection capacity, and to offset regulatory costs.
14 5-97-6. Penalties.
15 (a) Any individual who practices as an eyelash service technician without a valid license
16 issued pursuant to this chapter shall be subject to an administrative fine of five hundred dollars
17 ($500) per violation.
18 (b) Any facility or business owner operating an establishment offering eyelash services
19 without proper licensure or permit shall be subject to an administrative fine of five hundred dollars
20 ($500) per violation.
21 (c) Each day of continued noncompliance shall constitute a separate violation.
22 SECTION 3. This act shall take effect on January 1, 2027.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO BUSINESSES AND PROFESSIONS -- EYELASH SERVICES LICENSING
AND REGULATION ACT
***
1 This act would create a new license for eyelash service technicians, require permits for
2 eyelash businesses, establish training and exam standards, authorize RIDOH enforcement and
3 fines, and impose five hundred dollar ($500) penalties for unlicensed practice.
4 This act would take effect on January 1, 2027.
