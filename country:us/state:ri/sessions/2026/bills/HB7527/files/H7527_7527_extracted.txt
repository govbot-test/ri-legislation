A N A C T
RELATING TO EDUCATION -- EDUCATION MENTORSHIP PROGRAM ACT
Introduced By: Representatives Casimiro, McNamara, Potter, Read, Hopkins, Shallcross
Smith, Kazarian, Alzate, Diaz, and Stewart
Date Introduced: February 06, 2026
Referred To: House Education
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Findings.
2 The general assembly finds and declares that student mentorship programs are most
3 effective when the mentor has an established relationship with the student, an understanding of the
4 student’s educational, social, and personal background, and professional training in student support
5 and development. The purpose of this chapter is to ensure that education mentorship services
6 provided in public schools are delivered by qualified professionals who possess the training,
7 certification, and access necessary to support the academic, social, and emotional success of
8 students.
9 SECTION 2. Title 16 of the General Laws entitled "EDUCATION" is hereby amended by
10 adding thereto the following chapter:
11 CHAPTER 117
12 EDUCATION MENTORSHIP PROGRAM ACT
13 16-117-1. Short title.
14 This chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Education Mentorship Program Act".
15 16-117-2. Definitions.
16 For purposes of this chapter, the following terms shall have the following meanings:
17 (1) "Certified support educator" means an individual who holds a valid Rhode Island
18 educator certification or license in a student support discipline including, but not limited to:
19 (i) School guidance counselor;

1 (ii) School social worker;
2 (iii) School psychologist; or
3 (iv) Any other certified or licensed student support professional as determined by the
4 department of education.
5 (2) "Education mentor" means an adult assigned or designated by a school district or public
6 school to provide ongoing guidance, support, advocacy, and mentorship to a student or group of
7 students.
8 (3) "Public school" means any local education agency, school district, public charter
9 school, or state-operated school.
10 16-117-3. Qualifications of education mentors.
11 (a) Any education mentor providing mentorship services to students pursuant to this
12 chapter shall be a certified support educator.
13 (b) The education mentor shall, to the extent practicable:
14 (1) Have an existing professional relationship with the student; or
15 (2) Be assigned in a manner that allows sufficient time and access to develop a meaningful
16 and sustained relationship with the student.
17 (c) Education mentors shall have access to relevant student records, support plans, and
18 school-based resources, consistent with state and federal law, in order to effectively understand and
19 respond to the student's academic, social, emotional, and environmental needs.
20 16-117-4. Role and responsibilities of education mentors.
21 An education mentor under this chapter may:
22 (1) Provide individualized guidance and support aligned with the student's academic and
23 personal needs;
24 (2) Assist in identifying barriers to student success including, social, emotional, behavioral,
25 or environmental factors;
26 (3) Coordinate with educators, families, and community-based service providers, as
27 appropriate;
28 (4) Utilize evidence-based support strategies consistent with the mentor's professional
29 training and certification; and
30 (5) Advocate for appropriate interventions, accommodations, or services to support student
31 well-being and achievement.
32 16-117-5. Rules and regulations.
33 The department of education shall promulgate rules and regulations necessary to
34 implement the provisions of this chapter including, standards for mentor assignment, caseloads,

RELATING TO EDUCATION -- EDUCATION MENTORSHIP PROGRAM ACT
***
1 This act would require that student mentors in public schools be certified support educators,
2 ensuring mentors have professional training, established student relationships, and access to skills
3 and information needed to support student success.
4 This act would take effect upon passage.
