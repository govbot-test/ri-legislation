LC004541

S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
H O U S E R E S O L U T I O N
COMMEMORATING "NATIONAL INFERTILITY AWARENESS WEEK" ON APRIL 19-25,
2026, IN THE STATE OF RHODE ISLAND
Introduced By: Representatives McNamara, Shekarchi, Blazejewski, Chippendale,
Kennedy, Ajello, Tanzi, Santucci, Cortvriend, and Boylan
Date Introduced: April 16, 2026
Referred To: House read and passed
1 WHEREAS, According to RESOLVE: The National Infertility Association, 1 in 6 people
2 worldwide experience infertility, making it a prevalent public health concern that impacts
3 millions of families; and
4 WHEREAS, The World Health Organization and the American Medical Association
5 define infertility as a disease, recognizing that it can result from medical, genetic, environmental,
6 or unexplained causes; and
7 WHEREAS, Infertility affects women and men equally and does not discriminate based
8 on race, religion, sexual orientation, marital status or socioeconomic level; and
9 WHEREAS, The LGBTQI+ community also faces challenges when building their
10 families; and
11 WHEREAS, All people challenged in their family-building journey should have access to
12 all family-building options. Families facing obstacles while trying to start or expand can have
13 profound emotional, psychological and financial impacts, including stress, anxiety, depression,
14 and significant out-of-pocket expenses; and
15 WHEREAS, Family-building options include adoption, assisted reproductive
16 technologies, in vitro fertilization (IVF), third-party reproduction, and fertility preservation.
17 Access to these options is essential to ensuring reproductive equity for all individuals and
18 couples; and
19 WHEREAS, Cost and lack of insurance coverage are challenges for many in our State to

1 access the family-building option they need; and
2 WHEREAS, Raising awareness about infertility and the barriers faced by the family-
3 building community is the first step to removing these barriers, reducing stigma, supporting
4 affected individuals and families, and improving access to care; now, therefore be it
5 RESOLVED, That this House of Representatives of the State of Rhode Island hereby
6 joins RESOLVE: The National Infertility Association, dedicated volunteers, health care
7 professionals, and members of the infertility and family-building community in commemorating
8 and participating in “National Infertility Awareness Week” on April 19-25, 2026, in the State of
9 Rhode Island; and be it further
10 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of State be and hereby is authorized and directed to
11 transmit a duly certified copy of this resolution to CEO Danielle Melfi, RESOLVE: The National
12 Infertility Association.
