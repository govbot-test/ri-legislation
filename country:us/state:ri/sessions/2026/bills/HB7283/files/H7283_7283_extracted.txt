____________
A N A C T
RELATING TO PROBATE PRACTICE AND PROCEDURE -- COLLECTION AND
MANAGEMENT OF DECEDENT'S ESTATES
Introduced By: Representatives Chippendale, Newberry, and Place
Date Introduced: January 23, 2026
Referred To: House Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 33-9-1 of the General Laws in Chapter 33-9 entitled "Collection and
2 Management of Decedents’ Estates" is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 33-9-1. Return of inventory of estate.
4 (a) Every administrator and every executor, shall, within ninety (90) days after his or her
5 appointment or such longer period as may be allowed by the probate court, return to the probate
6 court, under oath, a true inventory of all the personal property, both tangible and intangible, and of
7 all claims, rights, causes of actions and other assets, other than real property, of the deceased, with
8 an appraisement thereof as of the date of the decedent’s death.
9 (b) Any administrator or executor, upon presentment of proof of appointment and personal
10 identification, shall have the authority to request, from any financial institution, all funds of the
11 decedent on deposit to be provided by the financial institution to the administrator or executor the
12 same day of the request, without incurring any additional fees.
13 (c) The department of business regulation shall have the authority to impose penalties for
14 violation of this section pursuant to § 19-14-26.
15 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
