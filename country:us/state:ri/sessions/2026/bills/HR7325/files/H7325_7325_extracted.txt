S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
H O U S E R E S O L U T I O N
PROCLAIMING JANUARY 27, 2026 TO BE “RHODE ISLAND FOOD INSECURITY
AWARENESS DAY” IN THE STATE OF RHODE ISLAND
Introduced By: Representatives Shekarchi, Blazejewski, Slater, Chippendale,
Paplauskas, Hull, J. Lombardi, Diaz, Kislak, and Voas
Date Introduced: January 27, 2026
Referred To: House read and passed
1 WHEREAS, In 2025, a record number of Rhode Islanders needed food assistance due to
2 high food prices, the steep rise in housing costs, and the end of COVID-19 relief programs; and
3 WHEREAS, According to the 2026 annual Status Report on Hunger in Rhode Island,
4 nearly one out of every five or 34% of Rhode Island households are unable to meet their basic
5 food needs; and
6 WHEREAS, Communities of color are at the greatest risk of hunger, such that while 27%
7 of white households are food insecure, the rate is 50% among Black households, 58% among
8 Latino households, and 44% among all other households (including Asian, Native American, and
9 more than one race/ethnicity); and
10 WHEREAS, In fiscal year 2025, the Rhode Island Community Food Bank (“Food Bank”)
11 distributed 18.5 million pounds of food through its 147 member agencies across Rhode Island;
12 and
13 WHEREAS, The Food Bank’s statewide network of 137 member agencies served an
14 average of 85,000 individuals per month in 2025 (January through September), an increase of 5%
15 from the previous year when an average 82,000 people were served monthly; and
16 WHEREAS, In fiscal year 2026 the Food Bank will spend $7 million on food acquisition
17 to keep pace with the high demand for food assistance; and
18 WHEREAS, The State of Rhode Island is committed to addressing the health and
19 wellbeing of its residents by continuing public awareness of increased food insecurity and

1 seeking ways to address this increased need; now, therefore be it
2 RESOLVED, That this House of Representatives of the State of Rhode Island hereby
3 proclaims January 27, 2026, to be “Rhode Island Food Insecurity Awareness Day” in the State of
4 Rhode Island; and be it further
5 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of State be and hereby is authorized and directed to
6 transmit a duly certified copy of this resolution to the Governor of the State of Rhode Island, The
7 Honorable Daniel J. Mckee.
