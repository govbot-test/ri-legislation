S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D

JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
A N A C T
RELATING TO EDUCATION -- HEALTH AND SAFETY OF PUPILS
Introduced By: Representatives Ackerman, Shekarchi, McNamara, Boylan, Finkelman,
Kazarian, Kennedy, Donovan, J. Brien, and Newberry
Date Introduced: January 15, 2026
Referred To: House Education
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 16-21 of the General Laws entitled "Health and Safety of Pupils" is
2 hereby amended by adding thereto the following section:
3 16-21-44. Bullying -- Directed community service.
4 (a) This section shall establish a form of restorative justice designed to address acts of
5 bullying (the "action") in and around schools that are based on religious beliefs, sexual orientation,
6 or ethnicity, in a manner which provides a suitable sanction for the action but also promotes
7 learning, knowledge, and insight to deter a student from engaging in future acts of bullying.
8 (b) As used in this section, the term "bullying" has the same meaning as "bullying" and
9 "cyberbullying" as defined in § 16-21-33.
10 (c) A student who commits an act or acts of bullying which is based upon or related to the
11 religious beliefs, sexual orientation, or ethnicity of the person subject to the bullying, may when
12 determined to be appropriate, in addition to any other penalties provided by law, be required to
13 perform up to ten (10) hours of directed community service in a setting and manner to both deter a
14 student from engaging in future acts of bullying, and also to promote learning, knowledge, and
15 insight. This community service may be imposed by a juvenile hearing board, or a school
16 administration, or any combination thereof. Directed community service imposed by a school
17 administration or juvenile hearing board shall not be deemed a criminal penalty.
18 (d) Directed community service shall be tailored to the bullying action. By way of
19 illustration only, and without imposing any limitations, for a student who has bullied a student

1 based upon the student’s Jewish faith or heritage, directed community service pursuant to this
2 section could be performed at a Holocaust museum.
3 (e) Any community service imposed pursuant to the provisions of this section shall need
4 to be completed within three (3) months from the date of imposition.
5 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO EDUCATION -- HEALTH AND SAFETY OF PUPILS
***
1 This act would provide a form of restorative justice, designed to address incidents of
2 bullying in and around schools, that is based on religious beliefs, sexual orientation, or ethnicity,
3 by utilizing directed community service tailored to the bullying action.
4 This act would take effect upon passage.
