A N A C T
RELATING TO THE ROGER WILLIAMS PARK ZOO -- 2026 BOND REFERENDA
Introduced By: Representatives Slater, Potter, and O'Brien
Date Introduced: March 25, 2026
Referred To: House Finance
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Proposition to be submitted to the people.
2 At the general election to be held on the Tuesday next after the first Monday in November
3 2026, there shall be submitted to the people ("people") of the State of Rhode Island ("state"), for
4 their approval or rejection, the following proposition:
5 "Shall the action of the general assembly, by an act passed at the 2026 legislative session,
6 authorizing the issuance of bonds, refunding bonds, and temporary notes of the State of Rhode
7 Island for the capital project and in the amount of $18,000,000 with respect to the project relating
8 to the Roger Williams Park Zoo listed below be approved, and the issuance of bonds, refunding
9 bonds, and temporary notes authorized in accordance with the provisions of said act?"
10 Approval of this question will allow the State of Rhode Island to issue general obligation
11 bonds, refunding bonds, and/or temporary notes in an amount not to exceed eighteen million dollars
12 ($18,000,000).
13 These projects funded with the bond proceeds will help Roger Williams Park Zoo make
14 investments to renovate existing exhibit space to allow guests to view African native species in
15 exciting new adaptive habitat spaces and convert the former education center into a year round
16 accessible first class climate controlled facility occupied by more than twenty-five (25) different
17 species of reptiles, amphibians and invertebrates. These projects will improve the visitor experience
18 and further contribute to the Zoo’s positive economic impacts on Roger Williams Park, the city of
19 Providence and all of Rhode Island.

1 SECTION 2. Ballot labels and applicability of general election laws.
2 The secretary of state shall prepare and deliver to the state board of elections ballot labels
3 for the project provided for in section 1 hereof with the designations "approve" or "reject" provided
4 next to the description of the project to enable voters to approve or reject the proposition. The
5 general election laws, so far as consistent herewith, shall apply to this proposition.
6 SECTION 3. Approval of project by people.
7 If a majority of the people voting on the proposition in section 1 hereof shall vote to
8 approve the project stated therein, said project shall be deemed to be approved by the people. The
9 authority to issue bonds, refunding bonds and/or temporary notes of the state shall be limited to the
10 aggregate amount for the project as set forth in the proposition, which has been approved by the
11 people.
12 SECTION 4. Bonds for capital development program.
13 The general treasurer is hereby authorized and empowered, with the approval of the
14 governor, and in accordance with the provisions of this act to issue capital development bonds in
15 serial form, in the name of and on behalf of the State of Rhode Island, in amounts as may be
16 specified by the governor in an aggregate principal amount not to exceed the total amount for the
17 project approved by the people and designated as "capital development loan of 2026 bonds."
18 Provided, however, that the aggregate principal amount of such capital development bonds and of
19 any temporary notes outstanding at any one time issued in anticipation thereof pursuant to section
20 7 hereof shall not exceed the total amount for the project approved by the people. All provisions in
21 this act relating to "bonds" shall also be deemed to apply to "refunding bonds."
22 Capital development bonds issued under this act shall be in denominations of one thousand
23 dollars ($1,000) each, or multiples thereof, and shall be payable in any coin or currency of the
24 United States which at the time of payment shall be legal tender for public and private debts. These
25 capital development bonds shall bear such date or dates, mature at specified time or times, but not
26 mature beyond the end of the twentieth state fiscal year following the fiscal year in which they are
27 issued; bear interest payable semi-annually at a specified rate or different or varying rates; be
28 payable at a designated time or times at a specified place or places; be subject to express terms of
29 redemption or recall, with or without premium; be in a form, with or without interest coupons
30 attached; carry such registration, conversion, reconversion, transfer, debt retirement, acceleration
31 and other provisions as may be fixed by the general treasurer, with the approval by the governor,
32 upon each issue of such capital development bonds at the time of each issue. Whenever the
33 governor shall approve the issuance of such capital development bonds, the governor's approval
34 shall be certified to the secretary of state; the bonds shall be signed by the general treasurer and

3 SECTION 5. Refunding bonds for 2026 capital development program.
4 The general treasurer is hereby authorized and empowered, with the approval of the
5 governor, and in accordance with the provisions of this act, to issue bonds to refund the 2026 capital
6 development program bonds, in the name of and on behalf of the state, in amounts as may be
7 specified by the governor in an aggregate principal amount not to exceed the total amount approved
8 by the people, to be designated as "capital development program loan of 2026 refunding bonds"
9 (hereinafter "refunding bonds").
10 The general treasurer with the approval of the governor shall fix the terms and form of any
11 refunding bonds issued under this act in the same manner as the capital development bonds issued
12 under this act, except that the refunding bonds may not mature more than twenty (20) years from
13 the date of original issue of the capital development bonds being refunded.
14 The proceeds of the refunding bonds, exclusive of any premium and accrual interest and
15 net the underwriters' cost, and cost of bond insurance, shall, upon their receipt, be paid by the
16 general treasurer immediately to the paying agent for the capital development bonds which are to
17 be called and prepaid. The paying agent shall hold the refunding bond proceeds in trust until they
18 are applied to prepay the capital development bonds. While such proceeds are held in trust, the
19 proceeds may be invested for the benefit of the state in obligations of the United States of America
20 or the State of Rhode Island.
21 If the general treasurer shall deposit with the paying agent for the capital development
22 bonds the proceeds of the refunding bonds, or proceeds from other sources, amounts that, when
23 invested in obligations of the United States or the State of Rhode Island, are sufficient to pay all
24 principal, interest, and premium, if any, on the capital development bonds until these bonds are
25 called for prepayment, then such capital development bonds shall not be considered debts of the
26 State of Rhode Island for any purpose starting from the date of deposit of such monies with the
27 paying agent. The refunding bonds shall continue to be a debt of the state until paid.
28 The term "bond" shall include "note," and the term "refunding bonds" shall include
29 "refunding notes" when used in this act.

4 All monies in the capital development bond fund shall be expended for the purposes

29 amphibians and invertebrates.
30 SECTION 7. Sale of bonds and notes.
31 Any bonds or notes issued under the authority of this act shall be sold at not less than the
32 principal amount thereof, in such mode and on such terms and conditions as the general treasurer,
33 with the approval of the governor, shall deem to be in the best interests of the state.

14 SECTION 8. Bonds and notes to be tax exempt and general obligations of the state.
15 All bonds and notes issued under the authority of this act shall be exempt from taxation in
16 the state and shall be general obligations of the state, and the full faith and credit of the state is
17 hereby pledged for the due payment of the principal and interest on each of such bonds and notes
18 as the same shall become due.
19 SECTION 9. Investment of monies in fund.
20 All monies in the capital development fund not immediately required for payment pursuant
21 to the provisions of this act may be invested by the investment commission, as established by
22 chapter 10 of title 35, entitled "state investment commission," pursuant to the provisions of such
23 chapter; provided, however, that the securities in which the capital development fund is invested
24 shall remain a part of the capital development fund until exchanged for other securities; and
25 provided further, that the income from investments of the capital development fund shall become
26 a part of the general fund of the state and shall be applied to the payment of debt service charges
27 of the state, unless directed by federal law or regulation to be used for some other purpose, or to
28 the extent necessary, to rebate to the United States treasury any income from investments (including
29 gains from the disposition of investments) of proceeds of bonds or notes to the extent deemed
30 necessary to exempt (in whole or in part) the interest paid on such bonds or notes from federal
31 income taxation.
32 SECTION 10. Appropriation.

34 pay the interest and principal due each year on bonds and notes hereunder is hereby annually

2 SECTION 11. Advances from general fund.

11 In carrying out this act, the director, or designee, is authorized on behalf of the state, with
12 the approval of the governor, to apply for and accept any federal assistance which may become
13 available for the purpose of this act, whether in the form of loan or grant or otherwise, to accept the
14 provision of any federal legislation therefor, to enter into, act and carry out contracts in connection
15 therewith, to act as agent for the federal government in connection therewith, or to designate a
16 subordinate so to act. Where federal assistance is made available, the project shall be carried out in
17 accordance with applicable federal law, the rules and regulations thereunder and the contract or
18 contracts providing for federal assistance, notwithstanding any contrary provisions of state law.
19 Subject to the foregoing, any federal funds received for the purposes of this act shall be deposited
20 in the capital development bond fund and expended as a part thereof. The director, or designee may
21 also utilize any private funds that may be made available for the purposes of this act.
22 SECTION 13. Sections 1, 2, 3, 11, 12, and 13 of this act shall take effect upon passage.
23 The remaining sections of this act shall take effect when and if the state board of elections shall
24 certify to the secretary of state that a majority of the qualified electors voting on the proposition
25 contained in section 1 hereof have indicated their approval of the project thereunder.
