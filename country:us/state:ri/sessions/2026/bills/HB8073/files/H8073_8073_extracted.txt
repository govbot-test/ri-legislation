S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
A N A C T
RELATING TO CRIMINAL OFFENSES -- UNLAWFUL SALE OF PROHIBITED
FIREARMS
Introduced By: Representatives Tanzi, Boylan, Alzate, Stewart, Kislak, Handy, Ajello,
and Giraldo
Date Introduced: February 27, 2026
Referred To: House Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 11-47.2-2 of the General Laws in Chapter 11-47.2 entitled "Unlawful
2 Sale of Prohibited Firearms [Effective July 1, 2026.]" is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 11-47.2-2. Restrictions on manufacture, sale, and purchase of prohibited firearms.
4 Restrictions on manufacture, sale, possession, and purchase of prohibited firearms. [Effective
5 July 1, 2026.]
6 (a) No person shall manufacture, sell, offer to sell, transfer, possess, or purchase a
7 prohibited firearm, except as otherwise authorized under this section. Any person convicted of
8 violating this subsection shall be punished by imprisonment of not more than ten (10) years, or by
9 a fine up to ten thousand dollars ($10,000), and the prohibited firearm shall be subject to forfeiture.
10 (b) The following limited exceptions to subsection (a) of this section shall apply:
11 (1) A person who lawfully possesses a prohibited firearm prior to July 1, 2026, may sell,
12 offer to sell, or transfer their prohibited firearm to a federally licensed firearm dealer, or to an
13 individual outside the state who may lawfully possess such weapon by December 31, 2026.
14 (2) A federally licensed firearm dealer may sell, offer to sell, or transfer a prohibited
15 firearm to another federally licensed firearm dealer, or to a law enforcement entity identified in
16 subsection (b)(3) of this section, or to an individual outside the state who may lawfully possess
17 such weapon.
18 (3) A law enforcement agency, acting under authority of the United States, the state, or any

1 of its political subdivisions, to import or purchase a prohibited firearm and may lawfully sell, offer
2 to sell, or transfer a prohibited firearm to a federally licensed firearm dealer or to another law
3 enforcement agency, acting under authority of the United States or a state or any of its political
4 subdivisions.
5 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
