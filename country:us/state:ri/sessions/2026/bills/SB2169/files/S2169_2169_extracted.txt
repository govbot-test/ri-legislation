S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
A N A C T
RELATING TO EDUCATION -- CAREER AND TECHNICAL EDUCATION
Introduced By: Senators Rogers, Paolino, E Morgan, and de la Cruz
Date Introduced: January 16, 2026
Referred To: Senate Labor & Gaming
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 16-45.1 of the General Laws entitled "Career and Technical
2 Education [See Title 16 Chapter 97 — The Rhode Island Board of Education Act]" is hereby
3 amended by adding thereto the following section:
4 16-45.1-4. Participation by students in training activities at the Rhode Island fire
5 academy.
6 (a) Effective July 1, 2026, students enrolled in an appropriately credentialed career and
7 technical education program in the areas of emergency medicine technician, firefighting, or public
8 safety (hereinafter collectively referred to as the “program”) including, but not limited to, students
9 under the age of eighteen (18) years, shall be eligible to participate in training activities at the Rhode
10 Island fire academy. The Rhode Island fire academy, in conjunction with the department of health,
11 the department of education, and the career and technical board of trustees, shall establish
12 guidelines and benchmarks a student shall meet within and, if appropriate, outside of the program,
13 in order to be eligible to participate in these training activities. Provided, as a prerequisite, a student
14 shall be deemed fit to participate in these training activities by both the student’s sending district
15 and the fire academy.
16 (b)(1) Effective July 1, 2026, students who successfully complete an appropriately
17 credentialed career and technical education program as described in subsection (a) of this section
18 may take the exams and skills testing for the following certifications:
19 (i) Hazardous materials awareness;

1 (ii) Firefighter I; and
2 (iii) Firefighter II.
3 (2) The fire education and training coordinating board established pursuant to § 23-28.2-
4 23 is authorized to promulgate any rules or regulations the board deems necessary to ensure
5 students are properly trained and fit to undertake the exams and skills testing for the certifications
6 referenced in this subsection.
7 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect on July 1, 2026.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO EDUCATION -- CAREER AND TECHNICAL EDUCATION
***
1 This act would provide that students enrolled in an appropriately credentialed career and
2 technical education program in the areas of emergency medicine technician, firefighting, or public
3 safety including, but not limited to, students under eighteen (18) years of age, could participate in
4 training activities at the fire academy. This act would also allow students who successfully
5 complete those secondary education programs to take certain exams and skills testing.
6 This act would take effect on July 1, 2026.
