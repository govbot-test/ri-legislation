A N A C T
RELATING TO EDUCATION -- RHODE ISLAND PREKINDERGARTEN EDUCATION
ACT
Introduced By: Senators Acosta, DiMario, Gallo, Murray, Mack, and McKenney
Date Introduced: January 23, 2026
Referred To: Senate Education
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 16-87-3 of the General Laws in Chapter 16-87 entitled "Rhode Island
2 Prekindergarten Education Act" is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 16-87-3. Planning phase for a prekindergarten program Rhode Island
4 prekindergarten program.
5 (a) The Rhode Island department of elementary and secondary education ("the
6 department") shall begin planning an initial, pilot maintain a prekindergarten program that meets
7 high quality standards, builds on the existing early childhood education infrastructure in the state
8 (including child care, Head Start and public schools) and serves children ages three (3) and four (4)
9 who reside in communities with concentrations of low performing schools. This planning phase
10 will develop specific goals to expand the pilot prekindergarten program over time and will also
11 identify opportunities to strengthen care and learning programs for infants and toddlers years.
12 (b) During this planning phase, Annually, the Rhode Island department of elementary and
13 secondary education will shall quantify the resources needed to achieve and maintain high quality
14 standards in prekindergarten programs and identify incentives and supports to develop a qualified
15 early education workforce, including opportunities for experienced early childhood educators and
16 paraprofessionals to acquire college degrees and earn early childhood teacher certification.
17 (c) The Rhode Island Annually, the department of elementary and secondary education will
18 begin to develop plans to shall collect and analyze data regarding the impact of the pilot state

1 prekindergarten program on participating children’s school readiness and school achievement.
2 (d) The department shall conduct a lottery to determine enrollment in the state
3 prekindergarten program, pursuant to § 16-87-3.1.
4 SECTION 2. Chapter 16-87 of the General Laws entitled "Rhode Island Prekindergarten
5 Education Act" is hereby amended by adding thereto the following sections:
6 16-87-3.1. Lottery for the Rhode Island prekindergarten program.
7 (a) As used in this section and § 16-87-3.2, the following words shall have the following
8 meanings:
9 (1) "Participating in the state prekindergarten program" means a municipality that receives
10 funding from the state to create or maintain slots in a classroom for three (3) or four (4) year-olds.
11 (2) "Preferred program" means a program(s) selected by the family of a potential
12 prekindergarten student when applying to enter the prekindergarten lottery. Families shall not be
13 permitted to identify their preferred program(s) from a list of prekindergarten programs operating
14 in any municipality participating in the state prekindergarten program.
15 (3) "Siblings" means students who are related by blood, marriage, or co-residence of their
16 parents.
17 (4) "Students who are economically disadvantaged" means students who qualify for free
18 or reduced lunch.
19 (b) To be eligible for the lottery, a student shall be:
20 (1) A resident of a community participating in the state prekindergarten program; and
21 (2) Four (4) years of age by September 1 of the lottery year.
22 (c) The prekindergarten lottery shall be conducted to consider the following:
23 (1) A family’s preferred program;
24 (2) Siblings that are already enrolled in a particular prekindergarten program; and
25 (3) The economic demographics of the municipality in which the prekindergarten program
26 operates.
27 (d) The Rhode Island department of elementary and secondary education shall promulgate
28 rules and regulations regarding the operation of the lottery for state-funded prekindergarten seats
29 pursuant to this section.
30 16-87-3.2. Enrollment at Rhode Island prekindergarten programs.
31 (a) Effective July 1, 2026, any prekindergarten program (a "program") located in any
32 municipality participating in the state prekindergarten program may accept students into their
33 program who are not residents of the municipality in which the program operates; provided that,
34 all of the following conditions are present:

1 (1) The program has open slots available;
2 (2) The student is otherwise eligible for the prekindergarten program; and
3 (3) The student is selected by the prekindergarten lottery.
4 (b) The acceptance of students into a program who are not residents of the municipality in
5 which the program operates shall not, in and of itself, cause a loss or reduction in state or local
6 education aid to the program.
7 (c) No student shall be excluded from a prekindergarten program who has a sibling enrolled
8 in that prekindergarten program based on the fact that the student lives in a different municipality.
9 (d) The Rhode Island department of elementary and secondary education shall promulgate
10 rules and regulations regarding the enrollment at state-funded prekindergarten seats pursuant to this
11 section.
12 SECTION 3. This act shall take effect upon passage.
