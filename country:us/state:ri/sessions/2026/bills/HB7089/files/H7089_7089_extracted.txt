1 17-14-4. Preparation of nomination papers for candidates — Combination of
2 endorsed candidates — Furnishing of nomination papers to candidates.
3 (a) Upon receipt of the declarations referred to in § 17-14-1, within two (2) business days
4 of the final date for filing endorsements, the secretary of state for statewide candidates and the local
5 board for general assembly and local candidates shall prepare nomination papers for each person
6 who has filed a declaration of candidacy as provided in § 17-14-1; provided that, in years when
7 there is a holiday during the declaration of a candidacy, endorsement, or nomination paper
8 preparation period, the nomination papers shall be furnished to candidates within one business day.
9 A minimum of three (3) sets of nomination papers shall be prepared for each candidate whose name
10 appears on nomination papers containing the name of a candidate for general assembly and for
11 local office. Nomination papers for voters from the city of Providence for the offices enumerated
12 in § 17-14-1 shall be furnished by the secretary of state. Nomination papers shall be prepared with
13 the name of the candidate as it appears on the voting list, notwithstanding that the candidate may
14 have signed their declaration of candidacy other than as the candidate’s name appears on the voting
15 list. Candidates for nomination for different offices endorsed by the appropriate committee of any
16 party shall be combined on the same nomination papers. The names of candidates for different
17 offices not endorsed by the appropriate committee of any party shall not be combined on the same
18 nomination papers. Nomination papers for candidates for general assembly shall be furnished by
19 the local board of the general assembly candidate. Nomination papers shall be furnished to the local
20 boards by the secretary of state and each shall bear the imprint of the state coat of arms and any
21 additional language required by law. Nomination papers shall be appropriately marked or color
22 coded to indicate the different political parties, the endorsed and unendorsed candidates of those
23 parties, and independent candidates.
24 (b) Candidates for nomination may, at their own expense, have nomination papers
25 duplicated in exact appearance and as prescribed in subsection (a) of this section.
26 (c) Nomination papers furnished by the secretary of state shall be personally issued to the
27 candidate, or in the case of combined nomination papers to one of the candidates, for whom they
28 were prepared, or to an individual presenting written authorization from the candidate, or one of
29 the candidates appearing on them, to receive the nomination papers.

34 for state and local office, on the twenty-eighth (28th) day before the primary, or in the case of

2 papers for independent presidential candidates and presidential candidates of political parties, other

18 (d) If any challenged signature is found to be invalid, for any reason in law, or forged, then

4 17-14-12. Filing of nomination papers.
5 All nomination papers for state offices or officers and all certified lists of candidates for
6 local offices or officers shall be filed in the office of the secretary of state (the certified lists by the
7 respective local boards), not later than fifty-four (54) fifty-five (55) days before the date of the
8 primary held to nominate candidates for general election; but when there is a primary to nominate
9 candidates for any office mentioned in § 17-15-7 to be voted upon at a special election for state and

11 before the twenty-sixth (26th) day preceding the day of the special primary election, or when there
12 is a primary to nominate candidates for any office listed in § 17-15-7 to be voted upon at a special
13 election for federal office, all nomination papers and lists of local candidates shall be filed in the
14 office on or before the forty-nineth (49th) day preceding the day of the special primary election.
15 Nomination papers for independent presidential candidates and presidential candidates of political
16 parties other than those defined in § 17-1-2(9) shall be filed in the office of the secretary of state
17 by the local boards of canvassers not later than fifty-four (54) sixty-one (61) days before the date
18 of the election.
19 17-14-13. Objections to eligibility of candidate or sufficiency of papers.
20 When nomination papers have been duly filed and are in apparent conformity with § 17-
21 14-11, they shall be conclusively presumed to be valid, unless written objections to them are made
22 as to the eligibility of the candidate or the sufficiency of the nomination papers or the signatures on
23 them. All objections for local candidates shall be filed at the local board by four o'clock (4:00) p.m.
24 on the fifty-sixth (56th) day before the primary. All objections for federal and state candidates shall
25 be filed in the office of the secretary of state or of the local board, as the case may be, by four
26 o’clock (4:00) p.m. on the next business day after the last day fixed for filing nomination papers in
27 the appropriate office as provided in this chapter fifty-fourth (54th) day before the primary. Nothing
28 in this section shall be construed to prevent the secretary of state or the local board, as the case may
29 be, from disqualifying a candidate based on the determination of the secretary of state or the local
30 board, acting on its own motion, that the candidate is ineligible or the nomination papers or the
31 signatures on them are invalid or insufficient.
32 17-14-14. Hearings on objections — Witnesses — Notice.
33 (a) All objections to nomination papers which are required to be filed with the secretary of
34 state shall be considered by the state board. The secretary of state shall, when requested by the state

1 board, immediately deliver to the board the nomination papers to which objection has been filed.
2 The state board may, at the hearing on the objections, summon witnesses, administer oaths, and
3 require the production of books, papers, and documents. The witnesses shall be summoned in the
4 same manner, be paid the same fees, and be subject to the same penalties for default as witnesses
5 before the superior court. A summons may be sworn to and an oath may be administered by the
6 board. When an objection has been filed, notice of the objection shall be immediately given by
7 registered or certified mail or by personal service by the state board to the candidates, addressed to
8 the residence of the candidate as given in the nomination papers, and to any party committee
9 interested in the nomination to which objection is made. The state board shall promulgate rules and
10 regulations on challenges to nomination papers filed by any candidate, an individual presenting
11 written authorization from the candidate, or the chairperson of any party committee that questions
12 the validity or authenticity of any signature on the nomination paper of a candidate.
13 (b) Additional notice may also be given by the state board if it sees fit to do so. The decision
14 of the board shall be rendered within two (2) days for nomination papers for candidates
15 participating in a primary election and thirty-five (35) days for nomination papers for candidates
16 not participating in a primary election, exclusive of Sundays and holidays, after filing of objections
17 and shall immediately be certified by the state board to the secretary of state.
18 (c) All objections to nomination papers other than state and federal candidates shall be
19 considered by the local board in the same manner and with the same effect as provided in subsection
20 (a) of this section for hearing of objections to nomination papers for state and federal candidates
21 by the state board.
22 SECTION 2. Section 17-15-1 of the General Laws in Chapter 17-15 entitled "Primary
23 Elections" is hereby amended to read as follows:
24 17-15-1. Date of primaries.
25 A primary election for the nomination of candidates for each political party shall be held
26 in each voting district in the manner provided in this chapter on the eighth ninth (9th) Tuesday
27 preceding biennial state elections. Provided, however, that if the ninth (9th) Tuesday is the day after
28 Labor Day, then the primary election shall be held on the next day, the Wednesday.
29 SECTION 3. This act shall take effect on January 1, 2028.
