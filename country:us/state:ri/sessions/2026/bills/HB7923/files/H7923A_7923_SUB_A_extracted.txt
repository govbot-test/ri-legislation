4 Unless the context otherwise requires, the words and phrases as defined in this section are

6 (1) “Administer” refers to the direct application of controlled substances to the body of a

5 law enforcement officer assigned by his or her qualified law enforcement agency to investigate
6 prescription drug diversion.

8 including, but not limited to: storage, retrieval, and processing of information.
9 (7) “Control” means to add a drug or other substance or immediate precursor to a schedule
10 under this chapter, whether by transfer from another schedule or otherwise.
11 (8) “Controlled substance” means a drug, substance, immediate precursor, or synthetic
12 drug in schedules I — V of this chapter. The term shall not include distilled spirits, wine, or malt
13 beverages, as those terms are defined or used in chapter 1 of title 3, nor tobacco.

15 prescription for an opioid analgesic.

15 5-37.4-2. Definitions.
16 For purposes of this chapter:
17 (1) “Chronic intractable pain” means pain that is: excruciating; constant; incurable, and of
18 such severity that it dominates virtually every conscious moment; and/or produces mental and
19 physical debilitation. A diagnosis and written documentation of chronic intractable pain made by a
20 physician licensed in the state of Rhode Island specializing in pain management, oncology, or
21 similar specialty defined in regulations shall constitute proof that the patient suffers from chronic
22 intractable pain.
23 (2) “Director” means the director of the department of health of the state of Rhode Island.
24 (3) “Intractable pain” means a pain state that persists beyond the usual course of an acute
25 disease or healing of an injury or results from a chronic disease or condition that causes continuous
26 or intermittent pain over a period of months or years. Unless the context clearly indicates otherwise,
27 the term intractable pain includes chronic intractable pain.
28 (4) “Practitioner” means healthcare professionals licensed to distribute, dispense, or
29 administer controlled substances in the course of professional practice as defined in § 21-28-
30 1.02(41).
31 (5) “Therapeutic purpose” means the use of controlled substances for the treatment of pain
32 in appropriate doses as indicated by the patient’s medical record. Any other use is nontherapeutic.
