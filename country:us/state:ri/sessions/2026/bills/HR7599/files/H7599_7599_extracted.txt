S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
H O U S E R E S O L U T I O N
RECOGNIZING FEBRUARY 10TH, 2026, AS "211 DAY" IN THE STATE OF RHODE
ISLAND AND HONORING UNITED WAY 211 FOR ITS SERVICE TO RHODE
ISLANDERS
Introduced By: Representatives Shekarchi, Blazejewski, Shallcross Smith, Fogarty,
Ajello, Kennedy, Edwards, Solomon, Tanzi, and Boylan
Date Introduced: February 10, 2026
Referred To: House read and passed
1 WHEREAS, United Way 211 is a free and confidential service available 24 hours a day,
2 365 days a year, connecting Rhode Islanders to essential health and human services, including
3 housing assistance, food access, mental health resources, and disaster response; and
4 WHEREAS, Since 2007, United Way of Rhode Island has operated and maintained 211,
5 providing vital support to individuals and families in need, ensuring access to services in nearly
6 200 languages and dialects; and
7 WHEREAS, United Way 211 has served as a critical communication and response
8 system during public emergencies, natural disasters, and public health crises, ensuring Rhode
9 Islanders receive timely and accurate information; and
10 WHEREAS, United Way of Rhode Island has handled nearly 100,000 needs in the State
11 in 2025, including referrals handled through 211 and the Aging Disability Resource Center; and
12 WHEREAS, The data and insights collected through 211 services help inform policy
13 decisions and resource allocation, allowing state and local leaders to better serve their
14 communities; and
15 WHEREAS, Recognizing February 10th as "211 Day" honors the vital contributions of
16 United Way 211 and the many community organizations that collaborate to improve the well-
17 being of Rhode Islanders; now, therefore be it
18 RESOLVED, That this House of Representatives of the State of Rhode Island hereby
19 proclaims February 10, 2026, as "211 Day" in Rhode Island, recognizing the significant

1 contributions of United Way 211 and its partners in serving the people of our State; and be it
2 further
3 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of State be and hereby is authorized and directed to
4 transmit duly certified copies of this resolution to the United Way of Rhode Island and other
5 relevant community partners.
