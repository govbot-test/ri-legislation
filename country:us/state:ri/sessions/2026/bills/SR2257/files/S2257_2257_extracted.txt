S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
S E N A T E R E S O L U T I O N
RESPECTFULLY REQUESTING THE EXECUTIVE OFFICE OF HEALTH AND HUMAN
SERVICES (EOHHS) TO ADOPT AN ELECTRONIC PREADMISSION SCREENING AND
RESIDENT REVIEW (PASRR) SYSTEM
Introduced By: Senators DiMario, DiPalma, Murray, Lauria, Zurier, Acosta, Valverde,
Gallo, and Vargas
Date Introduced: January 23, 2026
Referred To: Senate Health & Human Services
1 WHEREAS, The PASRR system is a federal requirement to evaluate individuals seeking
2 admission to Medicaid-certified nursing facilities for serious mental illness or
3 intellectual/developmental disabilities; and
4 WHEREAS, Most states have transitioned from paper-based submissions to an electronic
5 PASRR system, which is a digital platform used to manage the federally mandated screening
6 process. Rhode Island is one of the only states, if not the only state, to have not yet converted; and
7 WHEREAS, Benefits of adopting an electronic PASRR system include:
8 • Protects Vulnerable Individuals: It identifies people with SMI/IDD to prevent
9 them from being unnecessarily institutionalized in nursing facilities;
10 • Promotes Least Restrictive Settings: Ensures people are placed in the most
11 appropriate, least restrictive environment, often in the community, when possible;
12 • Supports Community-Based Care: Facilitates home and community-based
13 services (HCBS) for more independence and freedom;
14 • Informed Choice & Dignity: Offers individuals and families awareness of
15 alternatives to nursing homes, supporting person-centered care;
16 • Ensures Appropriate Services: Guarantees that necessary specialized mental
17 health or IDD services are identified and provided;
18 • Rebalances Long-Term Services: Helps states shift resources from institutional
19 care to community support; and

1 • Reduces Readmissions: By connecting individuals with needed supports, it
2 lowers the risk of hospital readmissions; now, therefore be it
3 RESOLVED, That this Senate of the State of Rhode Island hereby respectfully requests
4 the RI Executive Office of Health and Human Services to:
5 • Investigate the potential cost savings and advantages to entering into a
6 Memorandum of understanding (MUO) with neighboring states in implementing an electronic
7 PASRR system;
8 • Investigate potential grant funding and Federal Funding for the adoption of an
9 electronic PASRR system; and
10 • Establish and implement the electronic PASRR System no later than January 1,
11 2027; and be it further
12 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of State be and hereby is authorized and directed to
13 transmit a duly certified copy of this resolution to the Honorable Governor Daniel McKee, and
14 Richard Charest III, R.Ph, MBA, Secretary, Executive Office of Health and Human Services.
