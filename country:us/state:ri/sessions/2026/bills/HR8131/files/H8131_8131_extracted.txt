S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
J O I N T R E S O L U T I O N
CREATING A SPECIAL JOINT LEGISLATIVE COMMISSION TO STUDY RHODE
ISLAND'S METHOD OF FUNDING CHARTER SCHOOLS
Introduced By: Representatives Edwards, Casey, Bennett, Diaz, Fellela, Potter, Cotter,
McNamara, Messier, and Furtado
Date Introduced: February 27, 2026
Referred To: House Education
1 WHEREAS, On June 10, 2010, the General Assembly enacted major school funding
2 reform by creating the current education funding formula; and
3 WHEREAS, The funding formula established the method of funding charter schools; and
4 WHEREAS, The number of charter schools has increased over the past three decades to
5 24 charters encompassing 39 individual charter schools; and
6 WHEREAS, The increase in the number of Local Education Agencies (LEAs) through
7 charter school expansion has increased expenditures for central office administration impacting
8 resources available for classroom instruction; and
9 WHEREAS, The method of funding charter schools has not undergone a thorough review
10 in over a decade; now, therefore be it
11 RESOLVED, That a special joint legislative commission be and the same is hereby
12 created consisting of thirteen (13) members: three (3) of whom shall be members of the House,
13 not more than two (2) from the same political party, to be appointed by the Speaker of the House;
14 three (3) of whom shall be members of the Senate, not more than two (2) from the same political
15 party, to be appointed by the President of the Senate; one of whom shall be the Executive
16 Director of the Rhode Island Mayoral Academies (RIMA), or designee; one of whom shall be the
17 Executive Director of the Rhode Island League of Charter Schools, or designee; one of whom
18 shall be the Executive Director of the Rhode Island School Superintendents' Association, or
19 designee; one of whom shall be the President of the Rhode Island League of Cities and Towns, or

1 designee; one of whom shall be the President of the Rhode Island Federation of Teachers and
2 Health Professionals, or designee; one of whom shall be the President of the National Education
3 Association Rhode Island, or designee; and one of whom shall be the President of the Rhode
4 Island Association of School Committees (RIASC), or designee.
5 The purpose of said commission shall be to make a comprehensive study and assess
6 Rhode Island's method of funding charter schools. The commission shall also conduct a
7 comprehensive study and assess the amount of administrative costs in district charter schools,
8 independent charter schools, mayoral academy charter schools, and traditional school districts.
9 Forthwith upon passage of this resolution, the Speaker of the House and the President of
10 the Senate shall designate the chair of the commission, and at the call of the chair, the
11 commission shall meet not later than August 31, 2026.
12 The commission shall meet regularly and may solicit input from stakeholders, including
13 community members, educators, and financial experts.
14 Vacancies in said commission shall be filled in like manner as the original appointment.
15 The membership of said commission shall receive no compensation for their services.
16 The Joint Committee on Legislative Services is hereby authorized and directed to provide
17 suitable quarters for said commission; and be it further
18 RESOLVED, That the commission shall submit a final report of its findings and
19 recommendations to the Governor, the Speaker of the House of Representatives, and the
20 President of the Senate on or before April 1, 2027, and said commission shall expire on July 1,
21 2027.

2 commission whose purpose it would be to study Rhode Island’s method of funding charter
