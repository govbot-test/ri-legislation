A N A C T
RELATING TO COURTS AND CIVIL PROCEDURE--COURTS -- DISTRICT COURT
Introduced By: Senators Burke, LaMountain, Gallo, and Patalano
Date Introduced: March 04, 2026
Referred To: Senate Judiciary
(Judiciary)
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 8-8-2 of the General Laws in Chapter 8-8 entitled "District Court" is
2 hereby amended to read as follows:
3 8-8-2. Divisions.
4 (a) For the distribution of court business, the state is divided into four (4) divisions as
5 follows:
6 (1) Second division. The second division consists of the city of Newport and the towns of
7 Jamestown, Little Compton, Middletown, Portsmouth, and Tiverton. Appeals and transfers in cases
8 arising in the enumerated cities and towns of the second division shall be transmitted by the clerk
9 of the Second Division District Court to the clerk of the Newport County Superior Court.
10 (2) Third division. The third division consists of the city of Warwick and the city of
11 Cranston and the towns of Coventry, East Greenwich, Foster, Glocester, Johnston, Lincoln, North
12 Kingstown, North Providence, North Smithfield, Scituate, Smithfield, West Greenwich and West
13 Warwick. Appeals and transfers in cases arising in the enumerated cities and towns of the third
14 division shall be transmitted by the clerk of the Third Division District Court to the clerk of the
15 Kent County Superior Court.
16 (3) Fourth division. The fourth division consists of the towns of Charlestown, Exeter,
17 Hopkinton, Narragansett, New Shoreham, North Kingstown, Richmond, South Kingstown, and
18 Westerly. Appeals and transfers in cases arising in the enumerated cities and towns of the fourth
19 division shall be transmitted by the clerk of the Fourth Division District Court to the clerk of the

1 Washington County Superior Court.
2 (4) Sixth division. The sixth division consists of the cities of Central Falls, East
3 Providence, Providence, Pawtucket, Woonsocket and the towns of Barrington, Bristol, Burrillville,
4 Cumberland and Warren. Appeals and transfers in cases arising in the enumerated cities and towns
5 of the sixth division shall be transmitted by the clerk of the Sixth Division District Court to the
6 clerk of the Providence County Superior Court.
7 (b) In each division the place or places for holding court shall be designated by the chief
8 judge.
9 (c) The district court shall have a seal for each of its divisions, which shall contain the
10 words, “District court of the State of Rhode Island, division.”
11 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect two (2) months after passage.

2 fourth division to better balance case workloads among the divisions.
