A N A C T
RELATING TO PUBLIC UTILITIES AND CARRIERS -- MORATORIUM ON NET
METERING, LONG-TERM CONTRACTS AND SUBSIDIES FOR HEAT PUMPS
Introduced By: Senators de la Cruz, Rogers, and Raptakis
Date Introduced: March 04, 2026
Referred To: Senate Environment & Agriculture
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Title 39 of the General Laws entitled "PUBLIC UTILITIES AND
2 CARRIERS" is hereby amended by adding thereto the following chapter:
3 CHAPTER 36
4 MORATORIUM ON NET METERING, LONG-TERM CONTRACTS AND SUBSIDIES FOR
5 HEAT PUMPS
6 39-36-1. Definitions.
7 As used in this chapter the following words and phrases shall have the following meanings:
8 (1) “Heat pump” means any device that transfers heat using mechanical energy;
9 (2) “Long-term contract” means any contract in excess of five (5) years;
10 (3) “Net metering” means using electrical energy generated by an eligible net-metering
11 system for the purpose of self-supplying electrical energy and power at the eligible net-metering
12 system site;
13 (4) “Solar energy” means a form of renewable energy that harnesses the power of the sun
14 to generate electricity;
15 (5) “Wind energy” means a form of renewable energy that harnesses the power of the wind
16 to generate electricity.
17 39-36-2. Moratorium related to net metering, long-term contracts and subsidies.
18 (a) Notwithstanding any other provisions of the general laws to the contrary including, but

1 not limited to, chapter 26.4 of title 39, any right to renew or enter into any net metering contracts
2 that are regulated by state law and no customer sited and/or grid-connected power generated energy
3 contracts shall be permitted under any provision of the general laws.
4 (b) Notwithstanding any other provisions of the general laws to the contrary, including, but
5 not limited to the provisions of chapter 26.1 of title 39, the State of Rhode Island is hereby
6 prohibited from entering into any long-term contracts for the purchasing of solar or wind energy
7 with any energy provider.
8 (c) Notwithstanding any other provisions of the general laws to the contrary, including, but
9 not limited to, the provisions of chapter 27 of title 39, and notwithstanding any program utilized by
10 the office of energy resources for the State of Rhode Island, no state funds shall be used to subsidize
11 the purchase or installation of heat pumps provided by any utility or governmental agency.
12 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect on January 1, 2027.
