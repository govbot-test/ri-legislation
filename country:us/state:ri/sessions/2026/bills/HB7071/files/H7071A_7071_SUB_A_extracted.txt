S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D

JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
A N A C T
RELATING TO AGRICULTURE AND FORESTRY -- PROTECTION OF TREES AND
PLANTS GENERALLY -- RHODE ISLAND INVASIVE PLANT MANAGEMENT ACT
Introduced By: Representatives Boylan, Bennett, Kislak, Santucci, McGaw, Carson,
Speakman, Spears, Donovan, and Cortvriend
Date Introduced: January 14, 2026
Referred To: House Environment and Natural Resources
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 2-15 of the General Laws entitled "Protection of Trees and Plants
2 Generally" is hereby amended by adding thereto the following section:
3 2-15-5.1. Terrestrial invasive plants — Prohibition on importation.
4 (a) No person shall import, disperse, distribute, introduce, or sell, in the state any species
5 of non-native (exotic) invasive plants, as defined by the director. The director shall promulgate
6 rules and regulations governing the prohibition and its applicability. The prohibition and its
7 enforcement shall not become effective until the rules and regulations governing the prohibition
8 and its applicability take effect.
9 (b) Violations of this section shall be punishable by a fine of not more than five hundred
10 dollars ($500).
11 (c) The director shall maintain a listing of all prohibited plants pursuant to this section
12 together with a listing of all prohibited aquatic plants pursuant to § 20-1-26.
13 SECTION 2. Section 20-1-26 of the General Laws in Chapter 20-1 entitled "General
14 Provisions" is hereby amended to read as follows:
15 20-1-26. Freshwater invasive aquatic plants — Prohibition on importation and
16 possession.
17 (a) No person shall import, transport, disperse, distribute, introduce, sell, purchase, or
18 possess in the state any species of non-native (exotic) freshwater invasive aquatic plants, as defined

1 by the director. The director shall promulgate rules and regulations governing the prohibition and
2 its applicability. The prohibition and its enforcement shall not become effective until the rules and
3 regulations governing the prohibition and its applicability take effect.
4 (b) Violations of this section shall be punishable by a fine of not more than five hundred
5 dollars ($500).
6 (c) The director shall maintain a listing of all prohibited plants pursuant to this section
7 together with a listing of all prohibited terrestrial plants pursuant to § 2-15-5.1.
8 SECTION 3. This act shall take effect on January 1, 2027.
