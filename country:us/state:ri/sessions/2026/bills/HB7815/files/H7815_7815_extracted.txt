A N A C T
RELATING TO HEALTH AND SAFETY -- AUTOLOGOUS OR DIRECT BLOOD
DONATIONS
Introduced By: Representatives Lima, and Hopkins
Date Introduced: February 26, 2026
Referred To: House Health & Human Services
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Title 23 of the General Laws entitled "HEALTH AND SAFETY" is hereby
2 amended by adding thereto the following chapter:
3 CHAPTER 4.15
4 AUTOLOGOUS OR DIRECT BLOOD DONATIONS
5 23-4.15-1. Autologous or direct blood donations -- Requirements.
6 (a) For purposes of this section:
7 (1) "Autologous blood donation" means when a person donates the person's own blood for
8 personal use during a future medical procedure.
9 (2) "Direct blood donation" means when a person donates the person's blood specifically
10 for the use of another person during the other person's future medical procedure.
11 (b) A hospital or other healthcare facility licensed under this title 23 shall allow a person
12 on whom a medical procedure is to be performed at the facility to donate a healthcare
13 provider‑ordered autologous or direct blood donation before the medical procedure if the facility
14 performs blood donations.
15 (c) A blood bank that facilitates autologous or direct blood donations shall comply with a
16 healthcare provider's order prescribing an autologous or direct blood donation for a person, unless
17 the person donating blood is in ill health, has a heart condition that makes donating blood
18 inadvisable, the person weighs too little to donate blood, or the person cannot or should not donate

1 blood for any other reason.
2 (d) A blood bank may charge not more than their regular fee to facilitate an autologous or
3 direct blood donation ordered by a healthcare provider under subsection (c) of this section. The
4 blood bank may charge a fee to store the donated blood until it is used by the person meant to
5 receive it.
6 (e) The department of health shall promulgate all rules and regulations necessary to
7 implement this section.
8 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
