S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
H O U S E R E S O L U T I O N
PROCLAIMING APRIL 19-25 OF 2026 TO BE "MEDICAL LABORATORY
PROFESSIONALS WEEK" IN THE STATE OF RHODE ISLAND
Introduced By: Representatives Shallcross Smith, Shekarchi, Blazejewski, Chippendale,
Alzate, Solomon, Baginski, Furtado, Place, and Read
Date Introduced: April 14, 2026
Referred To: House read and passed
1 WHEREAS, Medical Laboratory Professionals Week began in 1975 as National Medical
2 Laboratory Week under the auspices of the American Society for Medical Technology; and
3 WHEREAS, The 2026 theme for Medical Laboratory Professionals Week is “Lab Story:
4 To Infinity and Beyond For Our Patients”. This theme highlights the expansive reach and crucial
5 role of lab professionals in patient care, diagnosis and treatment, highlighting their journey and
6 impact; and
7 WHEREAS, The health and well-being of all Rhode Islanders depends upon educated
8 minds and trained hands; and
9 WHEREAS, The practice of modern medicine at the exacting standards we now enjoy
10 would be impossible without the accurate and timely scientific tests performed daily in medical
11 laboratories across the state; and
12 WHEREAS, Maintenance of these standards and progress toward improvement in the
13 quality of laboratory services depends on the dedicated efforts of professional clinical laboratory
14 science practitioners; and
15 WHEREAS, Through this dedication, medical laboratory professionals in Rhode Island
16 and across the United States have made vital contributions to the quality of our nation's health
17 care; now, therefore be it
18 RESOLVED, That this House of Representatives of the State of Rhode Island hereby
19 proclaims April 19-25 of 2026 to be "Medical Laboratory Professionals Week" in the State of

1 Rhode Island in recognition of the essential contributions of these professionals; and be it further
2 RESOLVED, That this House furthermore thanks all of the dedicated laboratory
3 professionals in the State of Rhode Island who work tirelessly to protect the health and safety of
4 our State's residents; and be it further
5 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of State be and hereby is authorized and directed to
6 transmit duly certified copies of this resolution to the Director of the Rhode Island Department of
7 Health, Dr. Jerome Larkin, MD, Maddie Josephs, Chair, Allied and Rehabilitative Health at
8 CCRI, Leslie Martineau, Director, Laboratory Services, Wound Clinic and Sleep Disorder Center
9 at CharterCARE Health Partners, and Betsy Reilly, Retired Cytotechnologist and Laboratory
10 Manager at Women & Infants Hospital.
