1 SECTION 1. Section 45-53-4 of the General Laws in Chapter 45-53 entitled "Low and
2 Moderate Income Housing" is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 45-53-4. Procedure for approval of construction of low- or moderate-income housing.
4 [Effective January 1, 2026, inclusive of existing language in § 45-53-4.]
5 (a) Any applicant proposing to build low- or moderate-income housing may submit to the
6 local review board a single application for a comprehensive permit to build that housing in lieu of
7 separate applications to the applicable local boards. This procedure is only available for proposals
8 in which at least twenty-five percent (25%) of the housing is low- or moderate-income housing.
9 This procedure is not available in cities and towns that have low- or moderate-income housing in
10 excess of ten percent (10%) of its year-round housing units which also have an inclusionary zoning
11 ordinance which complies with § 45-24-46.1.
12 (b) Cities and towns that have low- or moderate-income housing in excess of ten percent
13 (10%) of its year-round housing units:
14 (1) May provide an applicant with more dwelling units than allowed by right under its
15 zoning ordinance in the form of a density bonus to allow an increase in the allowed dwelling units
16 per acre (DU/A), as well as other incentives and municipal government subsidies as defined in §
17 45-53-3; provided, however that, applications filed in the town of Tiverton shall not include
18 accessory dwelling units as part of any project;

1 (2) May, by council action, limit the annual total number of dwelling units in
2 comprehensive permit applications from for-profit developers to an aggregate of one percent (1%)
3 of the total number of year-round housing units in the town, and notwithstanding the timetables set
4 forth elsewhere in this section, the local review board shall have the authority to consider
5 comprehensive permit applications from for-profit developers, which are made pursuant to this
6 subsection, sequentially in the order in which they are submitted.
7 (c) Cities and towns that do not have low- or moderate-income housing in excess of ten
8 percent (10%) of its year-round housing units:
9 (1) Shall make available to applications under this chapter municipal government
10 subsidies, including adjustments and zoning incentives, to offset the differential costs of the low-
11 or moderate-incoming housing units. At a minimum, the following zoning incentives shall be
12 allowed for in these cities or towns for projects submitted under this chapter:
13 (A) Density bonuses. These cities and towns shall provide an applicant with more dwelling
14 units than allowed by right under its zoning ordinances in the form of a density bonus to allow an
15 increase in the allowed dwelling units per acre (DU/A). At a minimum, the following density
16 bonuses for projects submitted under this chapter, provided that the total land utilized in the density
17 calculation shall exclude wetlands; area devoted to roadway infrastructure necessary for
18 development; and easements or rights of way of record:
19 (i) For properties connected to public sewer and water, or eligible to be connected to public
20 sewer and water based on written confirmation from each respective service provider, the density
21 bonus for a project that provides at least twenty-five percent (25%) low- and moderate-income
22 housing shall be at least five (5) units per acre;
23 (ii) For properties connected to public sewer and water, or eligible to be connected to public
24 sewer and water based on written confirmation from each respective service provider, the density
25 bonus for a project that provides at least fifty percent (50%) low- and moderate-income housing
26 shall be at least nine (9) units per acre;
27 (iii) For properties connected to public sewer and water, or eligible to be connected to
28 public sewer and water based on written confirmation from each respective service provider, the
29 density bonus for a project that provides one hundred percent (100%) low- and moderate-income
30 housing shall be at least twelve (12) units per acre;
