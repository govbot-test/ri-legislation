A N A C T
RELATING TO HIGHWAYS -- RHODE ISLAND TURNPIKE AND BRIDGE AUTHORITY
Introduced By: Representatives Solomon, and Casey
Date Introduced: February 27, 2026
Referred To: House Finance
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 24-12 of the General Laws entitled "Rhode Island Turnpike and
2 Bridge Authority" is hereby amended by adding thereto the following section:
3 24-12-60. Safety barrier on bridges.
4 (a) The Rhode Island turnpike and bridge authority (RITBA) is hereby directed to design
5 a safety barrier and/or safety netting on the Mount Hope Bridge, the Claiborne Pell Bridge, the
6 Staff Sergeant Christopher Potts Sakonnet River Bridge, and the Jamestown Verrazzano Bridge
7 (the “bridges”). The process shall be implemented as follows:
8 (1) RITBA shall submit the design of the safety barrier and/or safety netting for the bridges
9 to the house finance committee and the senate finance committee on or before January 1, 2027.
10 (2) RITBA shall issue a request for proposals for the safety barrier and/or safety netting on
11 or before April 30, 2027.
12 (3) Proposals shall be reviewed and accepted by RITBA, and construction and installation
13 of the safety barrier and/or safety netting shall commence on the bridges, no later than August 1,
14 2027.
15 (b) Any newly constructed bridges within the state with a deck height of over one hundred
16 feet (100′) shall be required to have safety barriers or safety netting.

1 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO HIGHWAYS -- RHODE ISLAND TURNPIKE AND BRIDGE AUTHORITY
***
1 This act would direct the Rhode Island turnpike and bridge authority (RITBA) to design a
2 safety barrier or netting system on the Mount Hope Bridge, the Claiborne Pell Bridge, the Staff
3 Sergeant Christopher Potts Sakonnet River Bridge, and the Jamestown Verrazzano Bridge. The act
4 would include several deadlines to ensure construction commences no later than August 1, 2027.
5 Any newly constructed bridges with a deck height of over one hundred feet (100′) would be
6 required to have safety barriers or safety netting.
7 This act would take effect upon passage.
