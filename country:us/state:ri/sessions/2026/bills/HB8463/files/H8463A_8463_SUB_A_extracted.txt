A N A C T
RELATING TO CRIMINAL PROCEDURE -- ARREST
Introduced By: Representatives Read, Noret, J. Brien, Chippendale, Casey, Corvese,
O'Brien, Fascia, Santucci, and Cotter
Date Introduced: April 17, 2026
Referred To: House Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 12-7-10 of the General Laws in Chapter 12-7 entitled "Arrest" is
2 hereby amended to read as follows:
3 12-7-10. Resisting legal or illegal arrest Resisting legal or illegal arrest by peace
4 officer-Resisting directive of correctional officer.
5 (a)(1) It shall be unlawful for any person to use force or any weapon in resisting a legal or
6 an illegal arrest by a peace officer, if the person has reasonable ground to believe that he or she is
7 being arrested and that the arrest is being made by a peace officer.
8 (2) It shall be unlawful for any person in custody of the Rhode Island department of
9 corrections to use any force or weapon to resist a lawful request or directive by a corrections officer.
10 (i) As used in this section, "a lawful request or directive "means an order issued within the
11 scope of a correctional officer's official duties and consistent with state law and department
12 regulations.
13 (b) Any person violating the provisions of this section shall be punished by a fine of not
14 more than five hundred dollars ($500) or by imprisonment for not more than one year, or by both
15 fine and imprisonment.
16 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
