S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
A N A C T
RELATING TO TOWNS AND CITIES -- RETIREMENT OF MUNICIPAL EMPLOYEES
Introduced By: Representatives Santucci, Hopkins, Nardone, Paplauskas, Chippendale,
Place, Roberts, and Fascia
Date Introduced: February 12, 2026
Referred To: House Finance
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 45-21-54 of the General Laws in Chapter 45-21 entitled "Retirement
2 of Municipal Employees" is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 45-21-54. Reemployment of retired members.
4 Any retired member of the system is permitted to reenter the service of the system for not
5 more than seventy-five (75) ninety (90) working days or its equivalent of six hundred thirty (630)
6 working hours in a calendar year without interruption of pension benefits. Pension payments,
7 however, are suspended when that period is exceeded. This seventy-five-day (75) ninety-day (90)
8 rule shall not apply to police officers, as defined in § 28-9.2-3, for the purposes of their working
9 private details, paid for by a nongovernmental entity. If the retired member continues in service
10 beyond the seventy-five-day (75) ninety (90) working day period (with his or her annuity
11 temporarily suspended) the member is not eligible for pension credit for the additional service, nor
12 is the member required to make pension contributions for this service; provided, that any retired
13 member of the system is permitted to serve as an elected city or town council member or school
14 committee member and continues to be eligible for and receive the retirement allowance for service
15 other than that as a council member or school committee member.
16 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO TOWNS AND CITIES -- RETIREMENT OF MUNICIPAL EMPLOYEES
***
1 This act would increase the number of days a retired municipal employee could work in a
2 calendar year without interruption of pension benefits to ninety (90) days or its equivalent of six
3 hundred thirty (630) hours.
4 This act would take effect upon passage.
