A N A C T
RELATING TO COURTS AND CIVIL PROCEDURE -- COURTS -- PERMITTING
VIRTUAL OR REMOTE COURT HEARINGS
Introduced By: Senators Mack, Acosta, Kallman, Ujifusa, Bell, Quezada, Vargas, and
Euer
Date Introduced: March 04, 2026
Referred To: Senate Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Title 8 of the General Laws entitled "COURTS AND CIVIL PROCEDURE
2 — COURTS" is hereby amended by adding thereto the following chapter:
3 CHAPTER 20
4 PERMITTING VIRTUAL OR REMOTE COURT HEARINGS
5 8-20-1. Remote and virtual court appearances permitted.
6 Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, in all courts of the State of Rhode
7 Island including, but not limited to, the supreme court, superior court, family court, district court,
8 workers’ compensation court, and traffic tribunal, any hearing, conference, motion, proceeding, or
9 other court appearance may be conducted in whole or in part by virtual or remote means, as
10 permitted by the court.
11 8-20-2. Right to remote participation.
12 Any party to a proceeding including, but not limited to, a plaintiff, defendant, respondent,
13 witness, or attorney of record, shall be permitted to participate in and appear at any court proceeding
14 remotely by using a telephone or video conference connection, subject to reasonable rules and
15 procedures adopted by the court to ensure the orderly administration of justice.
16 8-20-3. Judicial discretion.
17 A court may require an in-person appearance upon a specific finding, stated on the record,
18 that a remote appearance would materially prejudice the rights of a party, impair the integrity of

1 the proceeding, or otherwise interfere with the fair and efficient administration of justice.
2 8-20-4. Equal legal effect.
3 An appearance made remotely pursuant to this section shall have the same force and effect
4 as an appearance made in person, and no party shall be disadvantaged or prejudiced solely because
5 such party or attorney appears remotely.
6 8-20-5. Rules and procedures.
7 The chief justice or presiding authority of each court may promulgate rules, administrative
8 orders, or procedures governing the use of remote or virtual appearances, including standards for
9 technology, notice, verification of identity, and the creation of an official record.
10 8-20-6. Accessibility.
11 Courts shall endeavor to ensure that remote participation options are reasonably accessible
12 to self-represented litigants and individuals with disabilities, consistent with applicable state and
13 federal law.
14 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO COURTS AND CIVIL PROCEDURE -- COURTS -- PERMITTING
VIRTUAL OR REMOTE COURT HEARINGS
***
1 This act would permit all Rhode Island courts to conduct hearings remotely and allow any
2 party or attorney to appear by telephone or video conference, with remote appearances having the
3 same legal effect as in-person appearances.
4 This act would take effect upon passage.
