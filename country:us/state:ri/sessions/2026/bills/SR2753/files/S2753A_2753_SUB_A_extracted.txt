S E N A T E R E S O L U T I O N
URGING THE GOVERNOR TO CONDUCT A VETERANS HOME WORKFORCE
ANALYSIS TO GUIDE POLICY AND BUDGET DECISIONS
Introduced By: Senators Ujifusa, DiPalma, Felag, Euer, Kallman, Appollonio, E
Morgan, Murray, Zurier, and Britto
Date Introduced: February 27, 2026
Referred To: Senate Special Legislation and Veterans Affairs
1 WHEREAS, The Rhode Island Veterans Home in Bristol is operating below licensed bed
2 capacity, including not being able to open a thirty-two (32) unit Foxtrot wing for almost six (6)
3 years, due to persistent workforce shortages affecting nurses, certified nursing assistants, and
4 support staff; and
5 WHEREAS, Operating below licensed capacity does not properly address the needs of
6 Rhode Island veterans, causes undue stress on families and healthcare workers, and reduces
7 federal per diem reimbursement and increases per-resident costs, compounding the fiscal and
8 human consequences of each year the crisis goes unresolved; and
9 WHEREAS, The staffing crisis has persisted in part because policy and budget proposals
10 have not been grounded in comprehensive analysis of what compensation and workforce
11 structures are actually required to recruit and retain qualified staff; now, therefore, be it
12 RESOLVED, That the Rhode Island Senate hereby urges and requests that:
13 (1) The Governor direct the Department of Administration to conduct a comprehensive
14 analysis of why the Veterans Home cannot hire and retain enough qualified staff to operate at full
15 capacity, examining factors including: wage competitiveness, shift differentials, retention
16 incentives, housing and commute barriers, scheduling flexibility and career advancement
17 opportunities;
18 (2) The Department of Administration, based on that analysis, develop a corrective action
19 plan including a timeline that identifies how to reach full occupancy; and

1 (3) The Governor's Fiscal Year 2027-2028 budget proposal for the Veterans Home reflect
2 and take steps to implement the corrective action plan's recommendations; and be it further
3 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of state be and hereby is authorized and directed to
4 transmit duly certified copies of this resolution to the Governor of Rhode Island, the Director of
5 Administration, the Director of the Office of Veterans Services, the Veterans Home
6 Administrator, and the Chairs of the Senate and House Finance Committees.
