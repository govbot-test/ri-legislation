S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
H O U S E R E S O L U T I O N
EXPRESSING CONDOLENCES ON THE PASSING OF SYLVIA A. DELUCA
Introduced By: Representatives Shekarchi, Blazejewski, Craven, Chippendale, McEntee,
Kennedy, Edwards, Fogarty, Fellela, and Ajello
Date Introduced: April 14, 2026
Referred To: House read and passed
1 WHEREAS, It is with deep sadness that this House has learned of the passing of Sylvia
2 A. DeLuca, of Saunderstown. She was the cherished wife of the late Honorable Anthony DeLuca,
3 with whom she shared a remarkable and loving marriage spanning sixty-six years, building a life
4 centered on devotion, partnership, and family; and
5 WHEREAS, Born in the City of Providence and raised in the City of Cranston, Sylvia
6 was a graduate of Cranston High School East and continued her education at Bryant College,
7 which is now known as Bryant University, laying the foundation for a life marked by diligence,
8 intelligence, and thoughtful care in all she pursued; and
9 WHEREAS, She began her professional career assisting in her brother’s jewelry business
10 as a bookkeeper, where her keen attention to detail and strong organizational abilities were
11 readily evident. She later dedicated many years of service as an Administrative Assistant at the
12 University of Rhode Island’s Bay Campus, where she earned the affectionate nickname “the key
13 lady” for her reliability, resourcefulness, and willingness to help others; and
14 WHEREAS, Sylvia was a woman of remarkable talents, who found great joy in cooking
15 and baking for her family, expressed her creativity through painting, and cultivated a deep
16 appreciation for art and design, particularly inspired by Asian aesthetics. Her thoughtful nature
17 and passion for bringing beauty into her surroundings were evident in all that she did, leaving a
18 lasting impression on everyone who knew her; and
19 WHEREAS, Above all, Sylvia’s life was defined by her unwavering love for her family,
20 in whom she took immense pride, and whose lives were enriched by her guidance, kindness, and

1 enduring presence; and
2 WHEREAS, She leaves behind her beloved children, Anthony A. DeLuca, Steven J.
3 DeLuca and his wife Kim, Luisa DeLuca and her wife, Brenda Harvey, along with her precious
4 granddaughters, Cara and Talia DeLuca; and
5 WHEREAS, Sylvia is also survived by her brother, John Mastrobuono and his wife
6 Carole, her sister-in-law Anna Spadetti, and many beloved nieces and nephews including
7 Stephanie Mastrobuono, Claudia Mastrobuono, Liza De Fedele, Marc Mastrobuono, Matthew
8 Mastrobuono, Jon Paul Mastrobuono, Peter J. Spadetti and Nancy Courtemanche; and
9 WHEREAS, Besides her husband, she was predeceased by her parents, John A. and Mary
10 Mastrobuono, her brother Paul E. Mastrobuono, and her brothers-in-law Ciro DeLuca and Peter
11 A. Spadetti; and
12 WHEREAS, Sylvia A. DeLuca will be remembered for her steady wisdom, generous
13 spirit, and the love she so freely gave, leaving behind a legacy that will live on in the hearts of her
14 family and all who were fortunate to know her; now, therefore be it
15 RESOLVED, That this House of Representatives of the State of Rhode Island hereby
16 expresses its deepest condolences and extends its heartfelt sympathy to the family of Sylvia A.
17 DeLuca; and be it further
18 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of State be and hereby is authorized and directed to
19 transmit a duly certified copy of this resolution to the family of Sylvia A. DeLuca.
