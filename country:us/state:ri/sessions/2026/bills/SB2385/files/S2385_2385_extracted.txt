JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
A N A C T
RELATING TO HUMAN SERVICES -- MEDICAL ASSISTANCE -- PRESCRIPTION
DRUGS
Introduced By: Senators Bissaillon, Murray, LaMountain, and DiMario
Date Introduced: January 30, 2026
Referred To: Senate Health & Human Services
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 40-21 of the General Laws entitled "Medical Assistance —
2 Prescription Drugs" is hereby amended by adding thereto the following section:
3 40-21-4. Prohibition of prior authorization or step therapy protocol.
4 (a) The Rhode Island medical assistance program, as defined under chapter 8 of this title,
5 and any contract between the Rhode Island medical assistance program and a managed care
6 organization shall not require prior authorization or a step therapy protocol for the coverage of a
7 medication classified as an anticonvulsant or antipsychotic on their drug formulary.
8 (b) If the secretary of the executive office of health and human services determines that
9 authorization from a federal agency is necessary for the implementation of this section, the
10 secretary is authorized to seek such state plan amendment and may delay implementing the
11 provisions until the authorization is granted.
12 (c) The Rhode Island medical assistance program shall require, through amending current
13 and future medical assistance managed care contracts, that the managed care organizations meet
14 the provisions of this section.
15 (d) The provisions of subsection (a) of this section do not affect clinical prior authorization
16 edits to prescriptions of medications classified as an anticonvulsant or antipsychotic.
17 (e) This section does not prevent the Rhode Island medical assistance program and any
18 contract between the Rhode Island medical assistance program and a managed care organization

1 from denying a medication that has been removed from the market due to safety concerns from the
2 federal Food and Drug Administration.
3 (f) For the purposes of this section, "step therapy protocol" means a protocol that
4 establishes a specific sequence in which prescription medications for a specified medical condition
5 are medically necessary for a particular enrollee and are covered under a pharmacy or medical
6 benefit by the Rhode Island medical assistance program or a managed care organization, including
7 self-administered and physician-administered drugs.
8 (g) On or before July 1, 2029, the Rhode Island medical assistance program and any
9 managed care organization that has a contract with the Rhode Island medical assistance program
10 shall collect and provide to the executive office of health and human services, in a form and
11 frequency acceptable to the secretary, a report that includes the following data for the years 2021-
12 2029:
13 (1) For anticonvulsants and antipsychotics;
14 (i) The annual gross amount spent;
15 (ii) The annual net amount spent after rebates;
16 (iv) The average annual cost by class for generic and non-generic anticonvulsant and
17 antipsychotic medications;
18 (v) The total number of anticonvulsant and antipsychotic medication claims;
19 (vi) The percentage of adherence to the preferred drug list ("PDL") for anticonvulsant and
20 antipsychotic medications;
21 (vii) The total number of prior authorizations submitted for nonpreferred anticonvulsant
22 and antipsychotic medications;
23 (viii) The total number of step therapy exceptions requested for nonpreferred
24 anticonvulsant and antipsychotic medications;
25 (ix) The total number of step edits per nonpreferred anticonvulsant and antipsychotic
26 medication;
27 (x) The percentages of prior authorization and step therapy approvals and denials; and
28 (xi) The generic anticonvulsant and generic antipsychotic medication utilization
29 percentages.
30 (h) On or before July 1, 2029, the director of the department of health shall collect and
31 provide to the executive office of health and human services, in a form and frequency acceptable
32 to the secretary, a report that includes the following data for the years 2021-2029 from the state and
33 from each of the thirty-nine (39) municipalities:
34 (1) Gross amount, non-aggregated by entity type, spent on services and personnel related

1 to mental health emergencies performed by the following entities:
2 (i) Ground ambulance transport;
3 (ii) Air ambulance transport;
4 (iii) Hospitals, including inpatient and emergency departments;

7 (i) On or before October 1, 2029, the executive office of health and human services shall

16 on December 31, 2029.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO HUMAN SERVICES -- MEDICAL ASSISTANCE -- PRESCRIPTION
DRUGS
***
1 This act would prohibit the Rhode Island medical assistance program, and any contract
2 between the Rhode Island medical assistance program and a managed care organization, from
3 requiring prior authorization or a step therapy protocol for the coverage of a medication classified
4 as an anticonvulsant or antipsychotic.
5 This act would take effect on January 1, 2027, and would sunset and be repealed on
6 December 31, 2029.
