A N A C T
RELATING TO BUSINESSES AND PROFESSIONS -- ELECTRICIANS
Introduced By: Representatives Furtado, Messier, Cruz, Stewart, and Potter
Date Introduced: February 27, 2026
Referred To: House Labor
(Dept. of Labor and Training)
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 5-6-4 of the General Laws in Chapter 5-6 entitled "Electricians" is
2 hereby amended to read as follows:
3 5-6-4. Board of examiners — Composition — Appointment of members — Policy-
4 making role.
5 (a)(1) There is created in the division of professional regulation, in the department of labor
6 and training, a state board of examiners of electricians that at all times consists of seven (7) eight
7 (8) qualified electors of the state as follows:
8 (i) A member of an electrical inspection department of any city or town with, at least, five
9 (5) years’ experience as an electrical inspector to represent the general public;
10 (ii) A qualified electrical contractor to represent employers;
11 (iii) A journeyperson electrician to represent labor;
12 (iv) The state fire marshal, who is ex officio, or his or her designee;
13 (v) A qualified employee of any electric utility company to represent the utility companies;
14 (vi) A journeyperson electrician to represent organized labor selected from a list of three
15 (3) persons submitted to the governor by the American Federation of Labor — Congress of
16 Industrial Organizations (AFL-CIO); and
17 (vii) A member of the Rhode Island Sign Contractors Association.; and
18 (viii) A Rhode Island licensed telecommunications contractor.
19 (2) On or before January 31, the governor shall annually appoint a member or members of

1 the board to succeed the member or members whose term is at that time expiring who shall serve
2 for four (4) years or until his or her successor is elected and qualified. Any vacancy that occurs in
3 the board from any cause shall be filled by the governor for the remainder of the unexpired term.
4 (b) The board has a policy-making role in the preparation and composition of the
5 examinations to be administered by the division. Subsequent to the administration of the
6 examinations, the board of examiners shall review the examinations to evaluate their effectiveness.
7 The board shall supervise the operation of the division in an advisory capacity in promulgating any
8 policy that is necessary to improve the operation of the division in their area of expertise. The
9 promulgation of that policy is subject to the approval of the director of the department. Members
10 of the board are subject to the provisions of chapter 14 of title 36.
11 (c) The director of labor and training has the power to revoke or suspend the certificate or
12 license or impose a fine upon the certificate or license holder, for all certificates and licenses issued
13 by the division of professional regulation, after a hearing before and upon the recommendation of
14 the board of examiners of electricians.
15 SECTION 2. Section 5-70-4 of the General Laws in Chapter 5-70 entitled
16 "Telecommunications" is hereby amended to read as follows:
17 5-70-4. Licensing authority.
18 (a) There is established a board of examination and licensing of telecommunications
19 systems contractors, technicians, and installers that shall be composed of seven (7) members. Five
20 (5) members of the board shall be telecommunications systems professionals who shall be qualified
21 for licensing under the provisions of this chapter. One of these members shall also hold, at the time
22 of appointment, an electrical contractor license, certificate A, issued by this state. Each of the four
23 (4) categories shall be represented by a minimum of one licensed professional within that category.
24 Each telecommunications professional member of the board shall be actively engaged in either/or
25 the design, installation, alteration, service, and testing of telecommunications systems, as defined
26 by this chapter, for ten (10) consecutive years immediately prior to appointment. One member of
27 the board shall be, at the time of appointment, an electrical inspector serving a city or town in this
28 state. One member of the board shall be, at the time of appointment, a designated representative of
29 the state building code commission who is knowledgeable in codes and regulations involving
30 telecommunications. Each member of the board shall be a qualified elector of the state for three (3)
31 consecutive years prior to appointment.
32 (b) Each member of the board shall be appointed by the governor with the advice and
33 consent of the senate to serve for a term of five (5) years and until his or her successor is appointed
34 and qualified. Not more than one member of the board shall be directly employed with or by any

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO BUSINESSES AND PROFESSIONS -- ELECTRICIANS
***
1 This act would transfer authority over the examination and licensing of
2 telecommunications professionals to the state board of examiners of electricians and would
3 amend the composition of that body to require the inclusion of a telecommunications
4 professional.
5 This act would take effect upon passage.
