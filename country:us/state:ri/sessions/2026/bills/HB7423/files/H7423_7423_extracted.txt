A N A C T
RELATING TO STATE AFFAIRS AND GOVERNMENT -- DEPARTMENT OF
ADMINISTRATION
Introduced By: Representatives Speakman, Spears, Potter, Cortvriend, Boylan, Carson,
McGaw, DeSimone, Cotter, and Fogarty
Date Introduced: January 30, 2026
Referred To: House Environment and Natural Resources
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 42-11 of the General Laws entitled "Department of Administration"
2 is hereby amended by adding thereto the following section:
3 42-11-10.2. Statewide wastewater capacity study.
4 (a) The administrative head of the division of statewide planning ("division"), shall within
5 available appropriations and in coordination with the department of administration, the department
6 of environmental management and the executive office of housing, conduct a statewide wastewater
7 capacity study.
8 (b) The study shall evaluate the capacity, flows, physical condition, regulatory compliance,
9 and vulnerability to natural hazards of publicly and privately owned wastewater infrastructure
10 throughout the state.
11 (c) In conducting the study, the division shall:
12 (1) Identify areas underserved by wastewater infrastructure;
13 (2) Identify existing and projected wastewater capacity limitations that constrain housing
14 production or economic development;
15 (3) Identify areas with excess wastewater capacity suitable for additional residential or
16 mixed-use development;
17 (4) Evaluate how wastewater infrastructure conditions affect housing affordability, density,
18 and location including assessment of the cumulative impact of adjacent developments and land use

1 applications; and
2 (5) Make recommendations for efficient and strategic investments in wastewater
3 infrastructure to support housing and economic development while protecting public health and the
4 environment.
5 (d) Not later than July 1, 2027, the division shall submit a written report of the findings and
6 recommendations to the governor and to the general assembly, including the house and senate
7 committees having jurisdiction over housing, municipal government, finance, and the environment.
8 (e) The administrative head of the division shall transmit the report to the state planning
9 council for consideration in updates to the state guide plan.
10 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
