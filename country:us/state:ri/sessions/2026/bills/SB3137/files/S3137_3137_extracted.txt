S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
A N A C T
RELATING TO EDUCATION -- TEACHERS' TENURE
Introduced By: Senator Louis P. DiPalma
Date Introduced: March 20, 2026
Referred To: Senate Labor & Gaming
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 16-13-5 of the General Laws in Chapter 16-13 entitled "Teachers’
2 Tenure" is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 16-13-5. Suspension for cause — Payment for period suspended.
4 (a) Section 16-13-4 shall not prevent the suspension of a teacher for good and just cause.
5 Prior to the suspension of a teacher as provided in this section, the school committee shall hold a
6 pre-suspension hearing to determine if a suspension is warranted, and at the pre-suspension hearing,
7 shall consider any available evidence and afford the teacher or his or her counsel an opportunity to
8 respond to that evidence. In the event a teacher is suspended or otherwise not permitted to perform
9 his or her duties prior to the presuspension pre-suspension hearing, then the teacher shall be paid
10 his or her regular salary during that period.
11 (b) Whenever a teacher is suspended by a school committee, the school committee shall
12 furnish the teacher with a complete statement of the cause(s) of the suspension and, upon request,
13 shall afford the teacher a hearing and appeal pursuant to the procedure set forth in § 16-13-4. If the
14 teacher shall be vindicated as a result of the hearing or any appeal the teacher shall be paid in full
15 for the period of suspension, and provided further, that during the period of suspension, all medical
16 and insurance benefits shall remain in full force and effect. However, if the teacher is placed on
17 suspension pending the outcome of a criminal investigation or prosecution, and convicted of a
18 felony, as defined in § 11-1-2, the teacher shall repay to the school district the amount of the salary
19 paid during the period of suspension, less any amounts paid to satisfy all taxes and any employee

1 benefits.
2 (c) Nothing contained in this section shall be construed to prohibit or at any time to have
3 prohibited a school committee in a municipality or regional school district with an elected school
4 committee, or the chief executive officer in a municipality with an appointed school committee,
5 from agreeing, in a collective bargaining agreement, to the arbitration of disputes arising out of the
6 suspension of a teacher pursuant to subsection (a) of this section.
7 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
