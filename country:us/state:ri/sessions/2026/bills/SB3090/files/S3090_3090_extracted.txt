A N A C T
RELATING TO BUSINESSES AND PROFESSIONS -- PATIENT PAYMENT
INFORMATION
Introduced By: Senators Britto, McKenney, and Bissaillon
Date Introduced: March 13, 2026
Referred To: Senate Health & Human Services
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Title 5 of the General Laws entitled "BUSINESSES AND PROFESSIONS"
2 is hereby amended by adding thereto the following chapter:
3 CHAPTER 37.9
4 PATIENT PAYMENT INFORMATION
5 5-37.9-1. Patient payment information -- Requiring electronic payment information
6 prohibited.
7 (a) No healthcare provider, as defined in § 5-37.3-12, shall require a patient to provide bank
8 account information, a credit card number, a debit card number or any other form of electronic
9 payment to be kept on file with the healthcare provider as a prerequisite to seeing the patient for an
10 office visit or providing any healthcare service to the patient.
11 (b) A violation of subsection (a) of this section shall be considered an unfair trade practice
12 pursuant to chapter 13.1 of title 6 ("deceptive trade practices").
13 (c) Nothing in this section shall be construed to:
14 (1) Affect a patient's obligation to pay for healthcare services; or
15 (2) Prohibit a healthcare provider from requesting, collecting or storing bank, credit or
16 debit card or other payment-related information if the patient agrees to provide such information.

1 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO BUSINESSES AND PROFESSIONS -- PATIENT PAYMENT
INFORMATION
***
1 This act would prohibit a healthcare provider from requiring patients to provide electronic
2 payment information to be kept on file as a condition to receiving treatment. The act would make
3 a violation of this section a deceptive trade practice.
4 This act would take effect upon passage.
