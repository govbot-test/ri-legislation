It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Sections 16-77-2.1 and 16-77-3.1 of the General Laws in Chapter 16-77
2 entitled "Establishment of Charter Public Schools [See Title 16 Chapter 97 — The Rhode Island
3 Board of Education Act]" are hereby amended to read as follows:
4 16-77-2.1. Definitions.
5 As used in this chapter, the following words shall have the following meanings:
6 (5)(1) “Board of regents” means the Rhode Island board of regents for elementary and
7 secondary education.
8 (2) “Bona fide labor organization” means a labor organization recognized under the
9 National Labor Relations Act or the provisions of chapter 7 of title 28 (“labor relations act”).
10 (4)(3) “Charter public schools” means district charter schools, independent charter schools,
11 or mayoral academies.
12 (6)(4) “Commissioner” means the Rhode Island commissioner of elementary and
13 secondary education.
14 (1)(5) “District charter schools” means schools created by existing public schools, groups
15 of public school personnel, public school districts, or a group of school districts.
16 (6) “Employees” means non-represented, non-management, and non-confidential
17 employees of a charter school.
18 (2)(7) “Independent charter schools” means schools created by: (I) Rhode Island nonprofit
19 organizations provided that these nonprofit organizations shall have existed for at least two (2)

1 years and must exist for a substantial reason other than to operate a school; or (ii) Colleges or
2 universities within the State of Rhode Island.
3 (3)(8) “Mayoral academies” means schools created by a mayor of any city or town within
4 the State of Rhode Island, acting by or through a nonprofit organization (regardless of the time said
5 nonprofit organization is in existence) to establish a mayoral academy as hereinafter described in
6 chapter 16-77.4 (“Mayoral Academies”). For purposes of this chapter the term “mayor” shall
7 include any elected town administrator.
8 (7)(9) “Sending school district” means the district where the student attending or planning
9 to attend a charter public school resides.
10 16-77-3.1. Legislative purpose.
11 (a) The purpose of this chapter is to provide an alternative within the public education
12 system by offering opportunities for entities identified in § 16-77-2.1 to establish and maintain a
13 high performing public school program according to the terms of a charter. The key appeal of the
14 charter school concept is its promise of increased accountability for student achievement in
15 exchange for increased school autonomy.
16 (b) Charter public schools are intended to be vanguards, laboratories, and an expression of
17 the on-going and vital state interest in the improvement of education. Notwithstanding the
18 provisions of this section or any law to the contrary, a charter school shall be deemed to be a public
19 school acting under state law and subject to the Age Discrimination Act of 1975, 42 U.S.C. § 6101,
20 et seq., title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, 42 U.S.C. § 2000d, et seq., title IX of the educational
21 amendments of 1972, 20 U.S.C. § 1681, et seq., § 794 of title 29, and part B of the Individuals With
22 Disabilities Education Act, 20 U.S.C. § 1411, et seq. All students and prospective students of a
23 charter school shall be deemed to be public school students, having all the same rights under federal
24 and Rhode Island law as students and prospective students at a non-chartered public school. These
25 charter public schools shall be vehicles for research and development in areas such as curriculum,
26 pedagogy, administration, materials, facilities, governance, parent relations and involvement,
27 social development, instructor’s and administrator’s responsibilities, working conditions, student
28 performance and fiscal accountability. It is the intent of the general assembly to create within the
29 public school system vehicles for innovative learning opportunities to be utilized and evaluated in
30 pilot projects. The provisions of this chapter are to be interpreted liberally to support the purposes
31 set forth in this chapter and to advance a renewed commitment by the state to the mission, goals,
32 and diversity of public education.
33 (c) It is the intent of the general assembly to provide opportunities for teachers, parents,
34 pupils, and community members to establish and maintain public schools that operate

1 independently as a method to accomplish all of the following:
2 (1) Improve pupil learning by creating schools with rigorous academic standards in all
3 basic areas of instruction for high pupil performance;
4 (2) Increase learning opportunities for all pupils, with special emphasis on expanded
5 learning experiences for pupils who are identified as educationally disadvantaged and at-risk;
6 (3) Encourage the use of innovative teaching methods;
7 (4) Create opportunities for teachers, including the opportunity to be responsible for the
8 learning program at the school site;
9 (5) Provide parents and pupils with expanded choices in the types of educational
10 opportunities that are available within the public school system;
11 (6) Hold the schools established under this chapter accountable for meeting publicly
12 promulgated, measurable, state and charter-based pupil academic results, and provide the schools
13 with a method to implement performance-based and/or other student-based accountability systems,
14 while providing a means to restrict the expansion of ineffective charter public schools; and
15 (7) Encourage parental and community involvement with public schools.
16 (d) No private or parochial schools shall be eligible for charter public school status, nor
17 shall a charter public school be affiliated in any way with a sectarian school or religious institution.
18 Any charter public school authorized by this chapter shall be nonsectarian and nonreligious in its
19 programs, admissions policies, employment practices, and all other operations. The board of
20 regents shall not approve a charter to a school whose overall operation or education program is
21 managed by a for profit entity.
22 (e) The commissioner is empowered to promulgate rules and regulations consistent with
23 this chapter, in conformance with chapter 35 of title 42, for the creation and operation of charter
24 public schools. These rules and regulations shall set forth the process for rescission of state approval
25 of a charter public school, including appropriate protections to ensure the continued provision of
26 education services to the students of the charter public school whose charter is rescinded.
27 (f) All charter public schools shall adhere to financial record keeping, reporting, auditing
28 requirements, and procedures as required by the Rhode Island department of education and in
29 accordance with federal and state laws and regulations.
30 (g) No more than thirty-five (35) charters shall be granted. At least one-half (½) of the total
31 number of charter public schools in the state shall be reserved for charter school applications which
32 are designed to increase the educational opportunities for at-risk pupils.
33 (h) Charter schools shall remain neutral regarding unionization of any of its employees by
34 establishing a “union neutrality clause” to be included in the charter upon the renewal of a certified
