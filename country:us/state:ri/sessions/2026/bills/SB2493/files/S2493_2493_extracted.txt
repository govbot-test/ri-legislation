JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
A N A C T
RELATING TO MOTOR AND OTHER VEHICLES -- MOTOR VEHICLE OFFENSES
Introduced By: Senators Raptakis, Patalano, Thompson, Appollonio, Famiglietti,
Tikoian, Burke, and Paolino
Date Introduced: February 06, 2026
Referred To: Senate Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 31-27-4 of the General Laws in Chapter 31-27 entitled "Motor
2 Vehicle Offenses" is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 31-27-4. Reckless driving and other offenses against public safety.
4 Any person who operates a motor vehicle recklessly so that the lives or safety of the public
5 might be endangered, or operates a vehicle in an attempt to elude or flee from a traffic officer or
6 police vehicle, or who commits one or more moving violations while traveling at least twenty-five
7 miles per hour (25 mph) shall be guilty of a misdemeanor for the first conviction and a felony for
8 the second and each subsequent conviction a felony and shall be imprisoned for not more than five
9 (5) years and fined not less than one thousand dollars ($1,000), nor more than five thousand dollars
10 ($5,000).
11 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO MOTOR AND OTHER VEHICLES -- MOTOR VEHICLE OFFENSES
***
1 This act would increase the penalties for reckless driving from a misdemeanor first offense
2 to a felony, with a penalty of up to five years in prison and a fine of between one thousand dollars
3 ($1,000) and five thousand dollars ($5,000).
4 This act would take effect upon passage.
