3 42-72-5. Powers and scope of activities.
4 (a) The department shall be the principal agency of the state to mobilize the human,
5 physical, and financial resources available to plan, develop, and evaluate a comprehensive and

1 seeking employment at the department as a social caseworker or child protective investigator. The
2 department shall be provided with funding for one full-time employee, or the equivalent, to support
3 the implementation of the hiring process. The process shall be in effect through March 15, 2026.
4 (1) Generally, the process shall include, but need not be limited to:
5 (i) Screening and reviewing candidates for eligibility criteria including education and
6 experience;
7 (ii) Administering the requisite civil service examinations;
8 (iii) Conducting in-person interviews;
9 (iv) Determining which applicants will be offered employment; and
10 (v) Determining the order in which employment offers will be given.
11 (2) Specifically, the process shall include, but need not be limited to, the following
12 elements:
13 (i) Eligibility criteria. Candidates must meet the minimum job requirements as defined in
14 the specification with social caseworker IIs and child protective investigators as approved by the
15 department of administration.
16 (ii) Civil service examinations.
17 (A) Examinations shall be offered by the department at least three (3) times per month to
18 individuals who meet the eligibility criteria and at times that shall include a weekend, a weekday,
19 and a weeknight option.
20 (B) The director shall determine the process and administration of the exam. The director
21 is not obligated to schedule an examination if there are no current applicants for the position
22 available by the deadline set by the director pursuant to this subsection.
23 (C) If an applicant does not pass the examination, the department shall notify the applicant
24 as soon as is practicable. Applicants wishing to re-take the examination are not eligible to do so
25 until sixty (60) days have passed from the date the notification was sent.
26 (iii) In-person interviews.
27 (A) Applicants who pass the civil service examination shall be invited to an in-person
28 interview.
29 (B) The interview shall be conducted by at least two (2) current employees of the
30 department.
31 (I) One of whom shall have a culturally or racially diverse background; and
32 (II) One of whom is currently in a supervisory role over social caseworkers or child
33 protective investigators for at least three (3) years.
34 (III) Satisfying the requirements of subsections (f)(2)(iii)(B)(I) and (f)(2)(iii)(B)(II) of this
LC004608 - Page 7 of 10

1 section does not necessarily require two (2) individuals. One individual may satisfy both
2 requirements.
3 (C) There shall be a good faith effort to accommodate the availability of the applicant and
4 the individuals on the panel when scheduling the interview.
5 (iv) Offering employment.
6 (A) Prior to offering employment, an applicant shall pass both the civil service exam and
7 the in-person interview. Nothing herein is a guarantee of employment to an applicant who meets
8 these criteria.
9 (B) Determining whether an applicant successfully completes the in-person interview shall
10 be based on criteria established by the director.
11 (I) The department of administration shall score the civil service exams and provide a
12 pass/fail listing of all candidates to DCYF within five (5) business days of receipt of the exams
13 from DCYF.
14 (II) The director may create a method of scoring interviews to provide objectivity and
15 uniformity when assessing applicants.
