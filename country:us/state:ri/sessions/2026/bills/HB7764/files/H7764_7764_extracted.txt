JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026

A N A C T
RELATING TO PROPERTY -- RESIDENTIAL LANDLORD AND TENANT ACT
Introduced By: Representatives Potter, Cruz, Voas, Stewart, Alzate, and Sanchez
Date Introduced: February 12, 2026
Referred To: House Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 34-18 of the General Laws entitled "Residential Landlord and Tenant
2 Act" is hereby amended by adding thereto the following section:
3 34-18-63. Determination of rent amount -- Use of algorithmic device prohibited.
4 (a) Definitions. For the purposes of this section:
5 (1) “Algorithmic device” means a device, software, tool, or model that uses one or more
6 computer-based algorithms, including machine learning or artificial intelligence, to perform
7 calculations concerning local or statewide rent amounts, occupancy trends, or other rental market
8 data for the primary purpose of advising, recommending, or determining the amount of rent a
9 landlord may charge, including adjustments to existing rent, that is not widely publicly accessible.
10 (2) “Nonpublic competitor data” means information that is derived from or about other
11 landlords, renters, occupancy, or pricing that is not widely available to the general public, including
12 but not limited to:
13 (i) Actual current rent prices for specific properties, units, or landlords collected from
14 nonpublic sources;
15 (ii) Occupancy rates, lease start/end data, or historical rental performance data obtained
16 from proprietary sources;
17 (iii) Data licensed or shared among participants in a competitive rental marketplace.
18 (3) “Rent” means the total consideration, fees, or charges a residential tenant is required to
19 pay for occupancy of a residential premises under any rental agreement, including concessions.

1 (b) Prohibition. Effective January 1, 2027, no landlord, property owner, property manager,
2 agent, or person acting on their behalf shall:
3 (1) Employ, use, knowingly rely upon, or cause another to employ, use, or rely upon an
4 algorithmic device that incorporates, uses, or was trained using nonpublic competitor data for the
5 purpose of determining, fixing, or suggesting the amount of rent to be charged to a residential
6 tenant; or
7 (2) Use an algorithmic device to determine any change in rent for a renewed lease term or
8 rent adjustment for an existing residential tenant.
9 (c) Exemptions. This section does not apply to:
10 (1) Rent or income limits established by federal, state, or local housing programs where
11 algorithmic tools are used solely to comply with program guidelines;
12 (2) Tools that produce only historical reporting of aggregate market data published no more
13 frequently than monthly, provided no individualized rent recommendations are made;
14 (3) Bona fide internal record-keeping or accounting systems that do not influence or set
15 rent amounts.
16 (d) Enforcement and remedies.
17 (1) Violations of this section shall be deemed an unfair or deceptive act or practice under
18 chapter 13.1 of title 6 ("deceptive trade practices").
19 (2) The Rhode Island attorney general, or any person aggrieved by a violation, may seek
20 relief including equitable relief and civil penalties, as appropriate, under § 6-13.1-5 et seq.
21 (3) The attorney general may promulgate regulations necessary for enforcement of this
22 section, including definitions, exemptions, recordkeeping requirements, and compliance processes.
23 (e) Severability. Should any provision of this section be held invalid, the remainder shall
24 remain in full force and effect.
25 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
