A N A C T
RELATING TO CRIMINAL OFFENSES -- LARGE CAPACITY FEEDING DEVICE BAN OF
2022
Introduced By: Senators Raptakis, Famiglietti, Appollonio, Tikoian, Burke, Felag,
Thompson, Patalano, Ciccone, and Dimitri
Date Introduced: January 23, 2026
Referred To: Senate Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 11-47.1-3 of the General Laws in Chapter 11-47.1 entitled "Large
2 Capacity Feeding Device Ban of 2022" is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 11-47.1-3. Large capacity feeding devices prohibited.
4 (a) No person, except for a federally licensed firearm dealer, shall manufacture, sell, offer
5 to sell, transfer, purchase, possess, or have under his or her control a large capacity feeding device,
6 except as otherwise authorized under this chapter. Any person convicted of violating the provisions
7 of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and punished by imprisonment of not more than
8 five (5) two (2) years, or by a fine of up to five thousand dollars ($5,000) one thousand dollars
9 ($1,000), and the large capacity feeding device shall be subject to forfeiture. For subsequent
10 offenses, any person convicted of violating the provisions of this section shall be guilty of a felony
11 and punished by imprisonment of not more than five (5) years or by a fine of up to five thousand
12 dollars ($5,000) and the large capacity feeding devices shall be subject to forfeiture.
13
14 (b) The provisions of subsection (a) of this section shall not apply to:
15 (1) Any person who, on June 20, 2022, lawfully possesses a large capacity feeding device;
16 provided that, within one hundred eighty (180) days of June 20, 2022, the person:
17 (i) Permanently modifies the large capacity feeding device such that it cannot hold more
18 than ten (10) rounds of ammunition;

1 (ii) Surrenders the large capacity feeding device to the police department in the city or town
2 where the person resides in accordance with the procedures for surrender of weapons set forth by
3 the police department or the Rhode Island state police, or, if there is no such police department or
4 the person resides out of state, to the Rhode Island state police; or
5 (iii) Transfers or sells the large capacity feeding device to a federally licensed firearm
6 dealer or person or firm outside the State of Rhode Island that is lawfully entitled to own or possess
7 such a feeding device.
8 (2)(i) Any law enforcement officer exempt under §§ 11-47-9 and 11-47-9.1; or
9 (ii) A retired law enforcement officer exempt under §§ 11-47-9 and 11-47-9.1 who is not
10 otherwise prohibited from receiving such a feeding device from such agency upon retirement, and
11 who has a permit to carry pursuant to § 11-47-18(b).
12 (3) An active duty member of the Armed Forces of the United States or the National Guard
13 who is authorized to possess and carry such a feeding device.
14 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
