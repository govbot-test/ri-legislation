____________
S E N A T E R E S O L U T I O N
CONGRATULATING AND RECOGNIZING THE STUDENTS AND FACULTY OF
THOMPSON MIDDLE SCHOOL FOR THEIR PARTICIPATION IN THE ANNUAL RHODE
ISLAND CIVICS DAY AND CELEBRATING THEIR AWARD-WINNING ACHIEVEMENT
Introduced By: Senators Euer, Dimitri, Sosnowski, Rogers, Urso, Raptakis, Appollonio,
McKenney, Felag, and Zurier
Date Introduced: June 10, 2026
Referred To: Recommended for Immediate Consideration
1 WHEREAS, On May 22, 2026, Generation Citizen New England hosted its annual in-
2 person Rhode Island Civics Day at the Rhode Island State House, gathering nearly 200 civic-
3 minded middle and high school students to act as community problem solvers and changemakers;
4 and
5 WHEREAS, This historic occasion marked the very first year that Thompson Middle
6 School participated in the Generation Citizen programming and proudly sent its students to the
7 State House to engage with state leaders and present their community-based civics projects; and
8 WHEREAS, Under the exceptional leadership, guidance, and dedication of their teacher,
9 Tara Mello, the students of Thompson Middle School embraced the principles of experiential
10 civics education, effectively preparing themselves to meet the civic literacy proficiency
11 requirements of our State; and
12 WHEREAS, The outstanding caliber of Thompson Middle School's debut participation
13 was highlighted by a student team winning the prestigious Top Middle School Award for their
14 project, demonstrating extraordinary initiative and community vision; and
15 WHEREAS, The specific projects and the exemplary students who participated in Rhode
16 Island Civics Day include:
17 • "Turning an Empty Space into a Community Place" (Winner of the Top Middle
18 School Project): Jadaleis Capo, David Plowden, and Liliana Pullice; and
19 • "School Substance Abuse Program": Jayden Clarke, Olivia Jimenez, and Teagan

1 Worthen; and
2 • "Student Voice -- Creating an 8th Grade Student Council": Bailee Martinelli,
3 Alondra Molina, and Yasmelyn Ramirez; and
4 • "Improving School Lunches": Alissia Flores-Pulsone, Bishop Hazard, and Joshua
5 Marrero; now, therefore be it
6 RESOLVED, That this Senate of the State of Rhode Island hereby heartily congratulates
7 the students of Thompson Middle School and their teacher, Tara Mello, for their inspiring
8 participation in the 2026 Rhode Island Civics Day and celebrates the Top Middle School Award
9 recipients for their exceptional work; and be it further
10 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of State be and hereby is authorized and directed to
11 transmit a duly certified copy of this resolution to Thompson Middle School Teacher, Tara Mello,
12 and the Principal of Thompson Middle School.
