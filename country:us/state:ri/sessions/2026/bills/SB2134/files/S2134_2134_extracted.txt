A N A C T
RELATING TO ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES -- REGULATION OF SALES
Introduced By: Senators Dimitri, Thompson, and Famiglietti
Date Introduced: January 16, 2026
Referred To: Senate Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 3-8-9 of the General Laws in Chapter 3-8 entitled "Regulation of
2 Sales" is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 3-8-9. Transportation of alcoholic beverages by underage persons.
4 (a) Any person who has not reached his or her twenty-first (21st) birthday and who operates
5 a motor vehicle upon the public highways, except when accompanied by a parent, legal guardian,
6 or another adult who is over the age of twenty-one (21) years and related, whether by blood,
7 adoption or marriage, to the operator within the following degree of sanguinity: brother, sister,
8 grandfather, grandmother, father-in-law, mother-in-law, brother-in-law, sister-in-law, stepfather,
9 stepmother, stepbrother, stepsister, half-brother, half-sister, uncle, aunt, great uncle or great aunt
10 and, knowingly having liquor or intoxicating beverages in any form in containers, opened or
11 unopened, in any part of the vehicle shall be guilty of a criminal violation. The words liquor and
12 intoxicating beverages, as used in this section, have the same meaning as defined in chapter 1 of
13 this title. This section does not apply to persons between the ages of sixteen (16) and twenty-one
14 (21) who are transporting unopened alcoholic beverages in the course of their employment. No
15 person who is under the age of twenty-one (21) shall operate a motor vehicle upon the public
16 highways, while knowingly having liquor or intoxicating beverages in any form in containers,
17 opened or unopened, in any part of the vehicle, except when accompanied by a parent, legal
18 guardian, or another adult who is twenty-one (21) years of age or older and related, whether by
19 blood, adoption, or marriage, to the operator. The words “liquor” and “intoxicating beverages”, as

1 used in this section, have the same meaning as defined in chapter 1 of this title. This section does
2 not apply to persons between the ages of sixteen (16) and twenty-one (21) who are transporting
3 unopened alcoholic beverages in the course of their employment.
4 (b) Any person who violates subsection (a) of this section shall be subject to the following:
5 (1) For a first offense, a fine of not more than two hundred fifty dollars ($250) andor have
6 his or her license to operate a motor vehicle suspended for not more than thirty (30) days;
7 (2) For a second offense, a fine of not more than five hundred dollars ($500) andor have
8 his or her license to operate a motor vehicle suspended for not more than ninety (90) days;
9 (3) For a third or subsequent offense, a fine of no less not more than five hundred dollars
10 ($500) nor more than nine hundred and fifty dollars ($950) andor have his or her license to operate
11 a motor vehicle suspended for one year not more than six (6) months.
12 (c) The original jurisdiction for enforcement of this section shall be exclusively in the
13 traffic tribunal.
14 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES -- REGULATION OF SALES
***
1 This act would make it unlawful for anyone under the age of 21 to operate a motor vehicle
2 upon the public highways, while knowingly having liquor or intoxication beverages in any form in
3 containers, opened or unopened, in any part of the vehicle, except when accompanied by a parent,
4 legal guardian, or another adult who is 21 years of age or older and related, whether by blood,
5 adoption, or marriage, to the operator.
6 This act would take effect upon passage.
