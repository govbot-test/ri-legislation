32 (i) The categories of consumer health data collected and the purpose for which the data is
33 collected, including how the data will be used;
34 (ii) The categories of sources from which the consumer health data is collected;

1 (iii) The categories of consumer health data that is shared;
2 (iv) A list of the categories of third parties and specific affiliates with whom the regulated

4 (v) How a consumer can exercise the rights provided in §23-101.1- 5.
5 (b) A regulated entity and a small business shall prominently publish a link to its consumer

16 a processor to process consumer health data in a manner that is inconsistent with the regulated

15 23-101.1-5. Consumer rights and requests -- Refusal -- Appeal.
16 (a) A consumer has the right to confirm whether a regulated entity or a small business is
17 collecting, sharing, or selling consumer health data concerning the consumer and to access such

20 mechanism that the consumer may use to contact these third parties.
21 (b) A consumer has the right to withdraw consent from the regulated entity's or the small
22 business's collection and sharing of consumer health data concerning the consumer.
23 (c) A consumer has the right to have consumer health data concerning the consumer deleted
24 and may exercise that right by informing the regulated entity or the small business of the consumer's
25 request for deletion.

27 consumer health data concerning the consumer shall:
28 (i) Delete the consumer health data from its records, including from all parts of the
29 regulated entity's or the small business's network, including archived or backup systems pursuant
30 to subsection (c)(3) of this section; and
31 (ii) Notify all affiliates, processors, contractors, and other third parties with whom the
32 regulated entity or the small business has shared consumer health data of the deletion request.
33 (2) All affiliates, processors, contractors, and other third parties that receive notice of a
34 consumer's deletion request shall honor the consumer's deletion request and delete the consumer

5 23-101.1-6. Data security practices.
6 A regulated entity, by January 1, 2027, and a small business, by April 1, 2027, shall:
7 (1) Restrict access to consumer health data by the employees, processors, and contractors
8 of such regulated entity or small business to only those employees, processors, and contractors for
9 which access is necessary to further the purposes for which the consumer provided consent or where
10 necessary to provide a product or service that the consumer to whom such consumer health data
11 relates has requested from such regulated entity or small business; and
12 (2) Establish, implement, and maintain administrative, technical, and physical data security
13 practices that, at a minimum, satisfy reasonable standard of care within the regulated entity's or the
14 small business's industry to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and accessibility of consumer
15 health data appropriate to the volume and nature of the consumer health data at issue.
16 23-101.1-7. Processors.
17 (a)(1) By January 1, 2027, for a regulated entity and April 1, 2027, for a small business, a
18 processor may process consumer health data only pursuant to a binding contract between the
19 processor and the regulated entity or the small business that sets forth the processing instructions
20 and limit the actions the processor may take with respect to the consumer health data it processes
21 on behalf of the regulated entity or the small business.
22 (2) A processor may process consumer health data only in a manner that is consistent with
23 the binding instructions set forth in the contract with the regulated entity or the small business.
24 (b) A processor shall assist the regulated entity or the small business by appropriate
25 technical and organizational measures, insofar as this is possible, in fulfilling the regulated entity's
26 and the small business's obligations under this chapter.
27 (c) If a processor fails to adhere to the regulated entity's or the small business's instructions
28 or processes consumer health data in a manner that is outside the scope of the processor's contract
29 with the regulated entity or the small business, the processor is considered a regulated entity or a
30 small business with regard to such data and is subject to all the requirements of this chapter with
31 regard to such data.

PRIVACY ACT
***
1 This act would create the sensitive healthcare information privacy act to protect
2 reproductive and gender-affirming care data. The act would require the holders of such data, with
3 certain exceptions, to obtain consent from a consumer before collecting the data and would prohibit
4 the sale of the data without the permission of the consumer. The act contains several enforcement
5 mechanisms to ensure compliance with the law, including an individual cause of action and
6 authority for the attorney general to bring enforcement of an action.
7 This act would take effect upon passage.
