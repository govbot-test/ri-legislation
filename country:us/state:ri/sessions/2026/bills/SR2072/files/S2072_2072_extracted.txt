S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
S E N A T E R E S O L U T I O N
EXTENDING CONDOLENCES ON THE PASSING OF THE HONORABLE THOMAS E.
HODGE
Introduced By: Senators Urso, Bissaillon, Appollonio, Dimitri, LaMountain, de la Cruz,
Acosta, Famiglietti, Lauria, and Rogers
Date Introduced: January 15, 2026
Referred To: Recommended for Immediate Consideration
1 WHEREAS, It is with sadness that this Senate has learned of the passing of the
2 Honorable Thomas E. Hodge, the husband of Jeanne Hodge, and the father of Sharon Chakoian
3 and her husband, Gary, and Michael Hodge and his wife, Annmarie. He was also the grandfather
4 of Torey Cotrone, Daniel Cotrone, Ryan Hodge and Gillain Hodge; and
5 WHEREAS, A lifelong resident of the City of Pawtucket, Mr. Hodge was a son of the
6 late William and Genevieve Hodge, and the brother of Genevieve White, William Hodge, Patricia
7 Bacon, Eileen Gray, and the late Mary Fitzpatrick and John Hodge; and
8 WHEREAS, For more than thirty years, Mr. Hodge owned and operated Tom Tux in
9 Pawtucket; and
10 WHEREAS, Dedicated to the community, Mr. Hodge had served on the Pawtucket City
11 Council for twenty-nine years, and was elected as a State Representative for the City of
12 Pawtucket on November 6th of 1990, serving for one term. In addition, he served on the
13 Pawtucket Arts Council as President, Secretary, and Treasurer for many years; and
14 WHEREAS, A very proud Irishman, Mr. Hodge was honored as the Grand Marshall of
15 the Pawtucket Saint Patrick’s Day Parade. He had also served on the Board for the ARC of
16 Blackstone Valley of Pawtucket for many years, on the Pawtucket Water Board, and as the
17 homeless liaison for the City of Pawtucket; and
18 WHEREAS, Mr. Hodge has been honored with many awards and distinctions including
19 the Veteran's of Foreign Wars Legislative Award, St. Raphael Academy Volunteer of the Year,

1 and United Cerebral Palsy Board Member of the Year; now, therefore be it
2 RESOLVED, That this Senate of the State of Rhode Island hereby extends sympathy and
3 condolences on the passing of the Honorable Thomas E. Hodge; and be it further
4 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of State be and hereby is authorized and directed to
5 transmit a duly certified copy of this resolution to Jeanne Hodge and Family.
