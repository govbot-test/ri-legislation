S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
H O U S E R E S O L U T I O N
CREATING A SPECIAL LEGISLATIVE COMMISSION TO STUDY AND PROVIDE
RECOMMENDATIONS ON THE WATER SUPPLY PLANNING FOR NEW HOUSING
DEVELOPMENTS IN RURAL COMMUNITIES
Introduced By: Representatives Cotter, Kennedy, Chippendale, McGaw, Casimiro, and
Edwards
Date Introduced: February 27, 2026
Referred To: House Municipal Government & Housing
1 WHEREAS, Rhode Island faces a housing supply shortage that impacts all residents,
2 especially those with low and moderate incomes; and
3 WHEREAS, Safe drinking water supplies in Rhode Island are abundant but not infinite or
4 equally available throughout the state; and
5 WHEREAS, Protecting and ensuring access to safe drinking water supplies to support
6 existing and future needs is a public health priority; and
7 WHEREAS, New construction and impervious surface expansion can impact local public
8 drinking water supply watersheds and the abundance and quality of drinking water for abutting
9 communities; and
10 WHEREAS, The development of housing needs to be balanced with protecting drinking
11 water resources; now, therefore be it
12 RESOLVED, That a special legislative commission be and the same is hereby created
13 consisting of eleven (11) members: three (3) of whom shall be members of the House of
14 Representatives, not more than two (2) from the same political party, to be appointed by the
15 Speaker of the House; one of whom shall be the Director of the Department of Environmental
16 Management, or designee; one of whom shall be the Executive Director of the Rhode Island
17 League of Cities and Towns or designee; one of whom shall be the manager of a water authority
18 serving a municipality of fewer than twenty-five thousand in population; one of whom shall be
19 the manager of a water authority serving a municipality of more than twenty-five thousand; one

1 of whom shall be a professional who specializes in the hydrology, environmental engineering
2 and/or public health compliance of water systems; one of whom shall be the executive director of
3 Rhode Island Housing or designee; one of whom shall be the Director of the Rhode Island
4 Department of Health or designee; and one of whom shall be the Chairperson of the Water
5 Resources Board, or designee.
6 In lieu of any appointment of a member of the legislature to a legislative study
7 commission, or any commission created by a General Assembly resolution, the appointing
8 authority may appoint a member of the general public to serve in lieu of a legislator.
9 The purpose of said commission shall be to make a comprehensive study and provide
10 recommendations for evaluating, planning for, and limiting the impacts of new housing
11 development on the safety, quality, and capacities of public water supplies and watersheds in
12 rural communities.
13 Forthwith upon passage of this resolution, the members of the commission shall meet at
14 the call of the Speaker of the House and organize and shall select a chairperson.
15 Vacancies in said commission shall be filled in like manner as the original appointment.
16 The membership of said commission shall receive no compensation for their services.
17 All departments and agencies of the state shall furnish such advice and information,
18 documentary and otherwise, to said commission and its agents as is deemed necessary or
19 desirable by the commission to facilitate the purposes of this resolution.
20 The Speaker of the House is hereby authorized and directed to provide suitable quarters
21 for said commission; and be it further
22 RESOLVED, That the commission shall report its findings and recommendations to the
23 House of Representatives on or before June 15, 2027, and said commission shall expire on July 1,
24 2027.
