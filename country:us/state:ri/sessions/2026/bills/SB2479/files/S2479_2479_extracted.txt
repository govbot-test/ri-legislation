A N A C T
RELATING TO STATE AFFAIRS AND GOVERNMENT -- LAW ENFORCEMENT
OFFICERS’ DUE PROCESS, ACCOUNTABILITY, AND TRANSPARENCY ACT -- ICE
OUT ACT
Introduced By: Senators Kallman, Mack, Acosta, Quezada, Euer, Felag, DiPalma, Gu,
Lauria, and DiMario
Date Introduced: February 06, 2026
Referred To: Senate Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 42-28.6 of the General Laws entitled "Law Enforcement Officers’
2 Due Process, Accountability, and Transparency Act" is hereby amended by adding thereto the
3 following section:
4 42-28.6-19. Prohibition against hiring immigration and customs (I.C.E.) officers.
5 (a) A law enforcement agency, as defined by § 42-164-2, shall not employ any
6 individual who was hired as a sworn officer of the United States Immigration and Customs
7 Enforcement Agency on or after January 20, 2025.
8 (b) This section applies prospectively only and does not apply to any individual
9 hired by the agency prior to the effective date of this section.
10 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect on October 1, 2026

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO STATE AFFAIRS AND GOVERNMENT -- LAW ENFORCEMENT
OFFICERS’ DUE PROCESS, ACCOUNTABILITY, AND TRANSPARENCY ACT -- ICE
OUT ACT
***
1 This act would prohibit any law enforcement agencies in the state from hiring any
2 individual who was hired as a sworn officer of the United States Immigration and Customs
3 enforcement Agency on or after January 20, 2025 and would apply prospectively to an individual
4 hired on or after October 1, 2026.
5 This act would take effect on October 1, 2026
