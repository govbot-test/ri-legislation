A N A C T
RELATING TO LABOR AND LABOR RELATIONS -- DIVISION OF PROFESSIONAL
REGULATION
Introduced By: Senators Thompson, Ciccone, Tikoian, Famiglietti, Quezada, Burke,
Urso, and Dimitri
Date Introduced: March 20, 2026
Referred To: Senate Labor & Gaming
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 28-22 of the General Laws entitled "Division of Professional
2 Regulation" is hereby amended by adding thereto the following section:
3 28-22-4. Retirement status license.
4 (a) Any person currently holding a license issued or overseen by the division of
5 professional regulation who has attained the age of sixty-five (65) may renew their license as a
6 retirement status license subject and pursuant to subsections (b) through (d) of this section.
7 (b) An applicant for a retirement status license shall submit their original license to the
8 division of professional regulation, along with a form, to be provided by the division of professional
9 regulation, for such classification, which shall contain a statement expressing the licensee’s status
10 as retired and acknowledging that the applicant shall not actively engage in the practice of the
11 occupation or trade for which a license was originally issued, while possessing a retirement status
12 license.
13 (c) A licensee issued a retirement status license shall not practice or offer to practice the
14 occupation or trade for which a license was originally issued. The division of professional
15 regulation and any licensing boards associated therewith are authorized to investigate violations of
16 this provision in the same manner and bearing the same penalties as the unauthorized or unlicensed
17 practice of a regulated occupation or trade.
18 (d) If the division of professional regulation issues a retirement status license pursuant to

1 this section, it shall return the original license submitted pursuant to subsection (b) of this section
2 to the applicant. Such original license shall bear a designation or be stamped “Retired”.
3 (e) The fee for a retirement status license shall be twenty dollars ($20.00).
4 (f) A licensee issued a retirement status license may restore such licensee’s original license
5 by submitting a form, to be provided by the division of professional regulation, requesting
6 reinstatement by paying the then-current annual fee for such license, and demonstrating completion
7 of the most recent continuing education requirements or any other continuing or ongoing training
8 requirements applicable to the license, as though the licensee had remained in active status during
9 the year immediately preceding reinstatement.
10 (g) The division or professional regulation may, for good cause shown, grant a retirement
11 status license to a person who does not meet the requirements of subsection (a) of this section.
12 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect on January 1, 2027.
