S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2026
____________
A N A C T
RELATING TO EDUCATION -- HEALTH AND SAFETY OF PUPILS
Introduced By: Representative Anthony J. DeSimone
Date Introduced: February 27, 2026
Referred To: House Education
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 16-21 of the General Laws entitled "Health And Safety of Pupils" is
2 hereby amended by adding thereto the following section:
3 16-21-44. Weapon detection systems in schools.
4 (a) Definitions. For purposes of this section:
5 (1) “Public entrance” means the main entrance to any school building, gymnasium, school
6 event facility, sporting event site and any site where a school hosts or participates in an event even
7 if off campus.
8 (2) “School” means any private or public institution of elementary or secondary (K-12
9 grade) education, accredited or recognized as a private or public school by the state department of
10 education, to include every charter school, independent school and mayoral academy as defined in
11 chapters 77.1 through 77.4 of title 16.
12 (3) “Weapon detection system” means a walk-through security technology or integrated
13 set of devices designated to detect, through electronic, visual or analytical means the presence of
14 weapons including, but not limited to, firearms and hazardous objects. Such system shall be capable
15 of detecting the presence of weapons irrespective of whether such weapons are carried by or
16 otherwise in the possession or control of an individual.
17 (b) Commencing January 1, 2027, every school, all school facilities and buildings, as well
18 as sponsored or associated school events, and school-involved sporting and athletic field events
19 and auditoriums shall have installed weapons detection systems at every public entrance. All public

1 entrances that do not have a weapons detection system installed shall be locked and/or equipped
2 with alarms in order to prevent entry from the outside, but shall remain operable from the inside to
3 comply with the fire safety code.
4 (c) In consultation and cooperation with the office of the attorney general, state law
5 enforcement and the Rhode Island department of education, shall provide training in the use and
6 maintenance of all weapon detection systems to school officials who shall use the equipment and
7 protocol for responding when contraband is detected.
8 (d) The Rhode Island department of education, shall promulgate rules and regulations to
9 implement the provisions of this section.
10 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
