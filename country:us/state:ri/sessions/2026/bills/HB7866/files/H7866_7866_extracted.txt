EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO INSURANCE -- UNFAIR CLAIMS SETTLEMENT PRACTICES ACT
***
1 This act would prevent an insurer from designating a motor vehicle a total loss if the cost
2 to rebuild or reconstruct the motor vehicle to its pre-accident condition is less than seventy-five
3 percent (75%) to eighty-five percent (85%) of the "fair market value". It would allow the consumer
4 to designate the motor vehicle a total loss when the seventy-five percent (75%) threshold is met but
5 less than eighty-five percent (85%) of the fair market value of the vehicle.
6 This act would take effect upon passage.
